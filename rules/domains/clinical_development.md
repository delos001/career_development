## 1. Tag Taxonomy

Authoritative source for the tag vocabulary used in this domain. `personal/knowledge/Experience_Inventory.md` entries must conform to these dimensions and values. Writes are validated against this taxonomy at entry time.

- **Capability:** Clinical Trial Execution | Risk-Based Monitoring & Quality | Data & Analytics | Quality & Compliance | Process Design & Optimization | Technology Strategy & Implementation | Vendor Management & Oversight | Organizational Design | Governance & Risk Management | Strategic Planning & Roadmapping | Change Management & Adoption | Stakeholder Management & Influence | Team Leadership & Development | Financial Management & Budgeting | AI Engineering & Development

- **Role Level:** IC | Manager | Senior Manager | Director | Senior Director | VP

- **Org Context:** Greenfield | Scaling | Turnaround | Mature/Enterprise | Independent | Volunteer

- **Outcome (OPTIONAL):** Capability Building | Quality Improvement | Risk Reduction | Efficiency Gain | Scalability/Growth Enablement | Cost Savings

- **Org Type (career breadth):** Biotech | Pharma | Technology | CRO | ARO | Academic/Research | Staffing/Consulting | Military | Pharmacy | Hospital | Clinic

Capability, Role Level, and Org Context are required on every entry. Outcome is optional; include only when a genuine organizational outcome is attached. Omit for breadth-evidence, routine-task, or "did X" entries. Do not invent outcomes; forced values pollute the archetype-driven filter. Capability and Outcome values are pipe-separated when multiple apply. Role Level and Org Context take one value per entry.

## 2. Domain Vocabulary

Common terms and acronyms: CDM, CDASH, SDTM, ADaM, eTMF, CRO, ARO, EDC, CTMS, IXRS/IRT, RBM, SOP, submission cycle, inspection readiness, quality planning, governance framework, operating model, risk-based monitoring, data acquisition, data quality, clinical data exchange.

**Framing conventions:**
- Regulatory context (GCP, 21 CFR Part 11, ICH-GCP) is implicit in "regulated clinical environments"; do not repeat in every achievement.
- Metric conventions favor cycle-based outcomes (submission cycles, rework cycles, adoption cycles) over elapsed-time measures.
- Scale signals favor portfolio scope (studies, programs, therapeutic areas), vendor scope (CROs, external partners), or functional scope (data management, clinical operations, biostatistics) over raw headcount.

## 3. Pack Selection Criteria

**Use when:** the user's career centers on pharma, biotech, or CRO clinical development functions; the Experience Inventory is populated primarily with entries tagged to clinical trial execution, clinical data management, clinical operations, or regulated-environment quality and governance; the active role search targets sponsor-side or CRO-side clinical functions.

**Do NOT use when:** the user's career is primarily in medical affairs without clinical operations exposure, commercial or market access roles, pure regulatory affairs, or adjacent life-sciences sectors (medical device, diagnostics) where vocabulary and framing diverge materially.

## 5. Archetype Calibration Reference

One STRONG example per archetype, Leadership voice, illustrating the archetype's framing pattern using domain vocabulary. IC framing is derivable by applying the active content rules file's IC register to the same pattern.

**Archetype 1 (Transformation & Strategy):**
> "Built a governed data acquisition capability from greenfield as portfolio scale outpaced decentralized execution, securing adoption across eight global study teams through structured change management. Achieved consistent right-first-time execution within three submission cycles."

**Archetype 2 (Data & Analytics):**
> "Redefined data quality planning across global clinical programs, replacing retrospective validation with proactive quality specifications and structured ownership models. Established a governance framework creating consistent accountability across study teams and enabling KPI-based oversight."

**Archetype 3 (Process & Operations):**
> "Eliminated a near-50% rework rate on external laboratory submissions by redesigning acquisition procedures, clarifying decision rights across three functional areas, and embedding governance oversight into downstream processes. Reduced rework to zero within two submission cycles."

**Archetype 4 (Platform & Technology):**
> "Identified that legacy macro-driven data processes could not scale with portfolio growth and carried increasing regulatory risk. Led cross-functional platform evaluation balancing change management impact against long-term scalability, driving the enterprise selection of Databricks to replace legacy infrastructure."
