# Clinical Development: Archetype 4 Platform & Technology (IC)

Pack content for Archetype 4 at IC level within the Clinical Development domain. Load alongside the archetype skeleton at `rules/archetypes/Archetype_4_Platform_Technology.md` and level rules at `rules/cv/content_rules_ic.md`.

---

## Match criteria

The role must center on one or more of:

- Clinical systems configuration, setup, or support: EDC, CTMS, IXRS/IRT, RBM tools, or document management systems
- Technology implementation participation: contributing to system builds, integrations, or deployments in a regulated clinical environment
- System testing, validation, or UAT in a clinical technology context
- Clinical domain expertise applied to technology work: translating operational requirements into system configurations, advising developers, or evaluating platform fit
- Data flow and interoperability at the system level: understanding how clinical data moves across platforms and contributing to the integrity of those flows

Critical distinction within Clinical Development: this archetype applies when the primary work is hands-on with clinical systems (configuring, implementing, testing, or supporting them). It is not correct for roles primarily about technology strategy or platform governance ownership (those are above IC level). Verify against Process & Operations IC if the role centers on individual process or SOP work without a technology component.

---

## Summary framing

- **Lead with:** clinical systems expertise, technology implementation experience, and domain knowledge in regulated clinical environments
- **Do NOT lead with:** technology strategy ownership, enterprise platform governance, or vendor ecosystem leadership
- **Register:** technically capable practitioner with deep clinical systems knowledge who implements, configures, supports, and improves technology platforms in regulated clinical environments
- **Required-element content:** years of experience in clinical technology environments (regulated pharma/biotech, sponsor or CRO); core capability signal (clinical systems implementation, EDC or CTMS expertise, system integration, data flow and interoperability, or technology configuration and support); evidence of technical or domain depth (specific systems, platforms, or implementation contexts handled); forward-looking statement connecting clinical systems and technology experience to the platform need implied by the job

## Inventory tag priorities

All queries for this archetype use compound tag combinations per the archetype skeleton's Compound Query Requirement. The Clinical Development inventory does not have a dedicated Platform & Technology capability tag; technology-related achievements are tagged under Capability: Technology Implementation, often with companion tags.

**High priority (must be represented; flag if fewer than two coverable):**

- Capability: Technology Implementation; Outcome: Capability Building OR Scalability/Growth Enablement (system implementations or configurations that delivered a new or materially improved operational capability)
- Capability: Technology Implementation; Outcome: Risk Reduction OR Quality Improvement (system contributions that reduced data risk, improved data integrity, or supported compliance)
- Capability: Technology Implementation AND Capability: Data & Analytics (platform or system work where domain knowledge of clinical data requirements was the differentiating contribution)

**Medium priority (include where space permits):**

- Capability: Technology Implementation AND Capability: Process Design & Optimization (technology contribution that directly enabled or required a process change)
- Capability: Technology Implementation AND Capability: Clinical Trial Execution (clinical domain depth applied to technology work; vendor-side or consultant technology roles are strong candidates here)
- Capability: Technology Implementation AND Capability: Stakeholder Management & Influence (translating clinical requirements for technical stakeholders or advising on system design)

**Use if space permits:**

- Capability: Technology Implementation AND Capability: Change Management & Adoption (only when the contribution was to adoption or rollout of a technology, not just its implementation)

**Avoid:**

- Capability: Technology Implementation entries without clinical domain context (generic IT or software implementation work without regulated clinical environment relevance)
- Organizational governance and strategy language
- Capability: Process Design & Optimization without a Technology Implementation tag (route to Process & Operations IC)

## Achievement framing language

Pattern for this archetype at IC level: technology problem, system need, or platform limitation; specific technical contribution or implementation work; outcome or impact on system performance, data quality, or operational capability. Technical detail is appropriate and expected at IC level. Specific systems, configurations, and integrations should be named where they explain the outcome. The outcome must still be stated in impact terms (what was enabled, what risk reduced, what capability delivered), not just what was configured.

---

## Calibration examples

**WEAK** (describes task without context or outcome):

> "Configured RAVE EDC system for new clinical studies, coordinating with data management and clinical operations to ensure system setup met protocol requirements."

**STRONG** (technology problem first, specific contribution, concrete outcome):

> "Identified that legacy macro-driven data processes could not scale with portfolio growth and provided clinical domain input to a cross-functional platform evaluation. Contributed to the enterprise selection of Databricks to replace legacy infrastructure by grounding the evaluation in operational data requirements."

**REFRAME** (same advisory work, contribution and impact clearly stated):

> "Translated field-level risk-based monitoring workflows into platform feature requirements for an RBM technology vendor, ensuring the resulting tool was grounded in field-level monitoring practice rather than abstract design assumptions."

## Technical Proficiencies content

For this archetype at IC level, Technical Proficiencies is the most important section after Professional Experience. Expand fully per the archetype skeleton's Section 6 and content_rules_ic.md:

- Clinical Systems (priority subsection): EDC platforms, CTMS, IXRS/IRT, RBM tools, document management, third-party data systems, modern data platforms
- Languages: R, Python, SQL, SAS, VBA
- Environments and platforms: Databricks, AWS, Git
- ML and analytics frameworks: Scikit-learn, TensorFlow, Keras
- Visualization tools: Spotfire, Power BI, Tableau

List completely and accurately per source documents. Do not compress or minimize at IC level.

## Content to de-emphasize

- Summary references to platform leadership or technology strategic direction
- Organizational scope inflation (enterprise-level technology accountability or executive technology advisory authority not present in source documents)
