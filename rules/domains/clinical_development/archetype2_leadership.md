# Clinical Development: Archetype 2 Data & Analytics (Leadership)

Pack content for Archetype 2 at Leadership level within the Clinical Development domain. Load alongside the archetype skeleton at `rules/archetypes/Archetype_2_Data_Analytics.md` and level rules at `rules/cv/content_rules_leadership.md`.

---

## Match criteria

The role must center on one or more of:

- Clinical data strategy: defining how data is acquired, governed, assessed for quality, and made decision-ready across a portfolio or organization
- Data governance: establishing standards, ownership structures, accountability models, and quality frameworks for clinical or operational data
- Analytics capability development: building or maturing the organization's ability to generate decision-relevant insight from clinical or operational data
- Data science enablement: establishing the infrastructure, processes, or governance that allows data science to operate reliably at scale in a regulated environment
- Analytics operating model design: defining how analytics functions are organized, resourced, governed, and measured

Critical distinction within Clinical Development: this archetype is for roles where the primary output is a data or analytics capability (a governed system, a decision-relevant process, a mature operating model). Verify against Platform & Technology if the role is primarily about building or owning a technology platform. Verify against Process & Operations if the role is primarily about process standardization where data is incidental.

---

## Summary framing

- **Lead with:** data strategy, governance, and analytics capability leadership in regulated clinical environments; not technical execution or individual analytical output
- **Do NOT lead with:** programming languages, machine learning methods, specific tools, or hands-on data science work
- **Register:** strategic data leader who builds decision-relevant, governed analytics capabilities at enterprise or portfolio scale; not an analyst who produces models and reports
- **Required-element content:** years of experience in regulated clinical environments (pharma/biotech sponsor or CRO); core capability signal (data strategy, analytics governance, data quality leadership, analytics capability maturity, or clinical data operating model); evidence of organizational or portfolio-scale impact (cross-functional governance, enterprise standards, multi-program data ecosystems, executive-level influence on data decisions); forward-looking statement connecting data strategy and governance track record to the analytical challenge or maturity need implied by the job

## Inventory tag priorities

**High priority (must be represented; flag if fewer than two coverable):**

- Capability: Data & Analytics; Role Level: Senior Manager, Director, or Senior Director; Outcome: Capability Building
- Capability: Governance & Risk Management AND Capability: Data & Analytics; Outcome: Quality Improvement OR Capability Building
- Capability: Strategic Planning & Roadmapping AND Capability: Data & Analytics; Context: Scaling OR Mature/Enterprise
- Outcome: Capability Building; Context: Greenfield OR Scaling (at least one achievement must show building or materially maturing an analytics capability, not maintaining one)

**Medium priority (include where space permits):**

- Capability: Organizational Design AND Capability: Data & Analytics (analytics function structure, role definition, accountability models)
- Capability: Process Design & Optimization AND Capability: Data & Analytics (only when the process directly governed how data was assessed, qualified, or made decision-ready)
- Capability: Stakeholder Management & Influence; Role Level: Director or Senior Director (executive influence on data strategy or governance)

**Use if space permits:**

- Capability: Technology Implementation AND Capability: Data & Analytics (only when the technology decision was fundamentally a data strategy decision; achievement must still lead with data or governance outcome)
- Capability: Change Management & Adoption (only when adoption of a new data standard, governance model, or analytics operating practice required change management at scale)

**Avoid:**

- Hands-on analytical work as standalone achievement (model builds, script development, dashboard creation without organizational or governance outcome)
- Technical tool achievements without data strategy or governance framing
- IC-level data science work without governance reframing
- Capability: Process Design & Optimization without a Data & Analytics tag (route to Process & Operations)

---

## Achievement framing language

Pattern for this archetype at Leadership level: data quality risk, governance gap, or analytical maturity problem; governance/strategy/capability solution; decision-relevant or organizational outcome. The data problem must come first. Do not lead with the technical solution or the artifact produced. The outcome must describe organizational impact (what decisions improved, what risks reduced, what scale enabled), not technical performance metrics.

---

## Calibration examples

**WEAK** (leads with technical execution, no governance or strategy framing):

> "Developed R-based data transformation and aggregation procedures to enhance data review and compliance monitoring, investigating issue root causes and identifying data quality risks."

**STRONG** (data quality problem first, governance solution, organizational outcome):

> "Redefined data quality planning across global clinical programs, replacing retrospective validation with proactive quality specifications and structured ownership models. Established a governance framework creating consistent accountability across study teams and enabling KPI-based oversight."

**REFRAME** (same technical work repositioned as data strategy leadership):

> "Identified a systemic data stability risk in point-in-time freeze controls that could not detect meaningful change in longitudinally evolving datasets. Replaced the legacy model with a continuous risk-based surveillance capability that scaled across programs and reduced rework burden on study teams."

List format only. Exclude proficiency level descriptors, first-appearance dates, and application narratives. Section remains last in the document.

## Content to de-emphasize

- IC-level analytical work in recent roles: reframe at governance and strategy level or exclude
- Clinical operations management achievements (trial execution, site management, CRO oversight): earlier career context only for this archetype unless they directly informed a data strategy or governance decision
- Process standardization without data governance framing (route to Process & Operations)
- Summary mentions of specific tools or languages (belong in Technical Proficiencies only)
