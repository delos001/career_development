# Clinical Development: Archetype 2 Data & Analytics (IC)

Pack content for Archetype 2 at IC level within the Clinical Development domain. Load alongside the archetype skeleton at `rules/archetypes/Archetype_2_Data_Analytics.md` and level rules at `rules/cv/content_rules_ic.md`.

---

## Match criteria

The role must center on one or more of:

- Hands-on clinical data management, data quality review, or data cleaning in regulated environments
- Individual analytical delivery: building models, writing scripts, producing reports, or performing statistical analysis
- Data science application: applying ML, statistical, or computational methods to clinical or operational data problems
- Data quality monitoring, issue identification, and root cause analysis at the study or program level
- Regulatory data compliance: CDISC standards, SDTM/ADaM mapping, submission readiness at the dataset or deliverable level

Critical distinction within Clinical Development: this archetype applies when the primary output is analytical work, data deliverables, or data quality execution; not data strategy or governance ownership. If the role is primarily about building or governing an analytics capability at organizational scale, it may be above IC level. Verify against Platform & Technology IC if the role is primarily about technology platform configuration.

---

## Summary framing

- **Lead with:** clinical data expertise, analytical depth, and technical execution in regulated environments
- **Do NOT lead with:** data strategy ownership, enterprise governance authority, or analytics capability leadership
- **Register:** technically capable practitioner who delivers reliable, decision-relevant analytical work in regulated clinical environments; grounded in both domain knowledge and technical execution
- **Required-element content:** years of experience in regulated clinical environments (pharma/biotech); core capability signal (clinical data management, data quality, analytical delivery, data science application, or regulatory data compliance); evidence of technical or domain depth (specific methods, environments, or problem types handled); forward-looking statement connecting analytical and clinical data experience to the technical need implied by the job

## Inventory tag priorities

**High priority (must be represented; flag if fewer than two coverable):**

- Capability: Data & Analytics; Outcome: Quality Improvement OR Risk Reduction
- Capability: Data & Analytics; Outcome: Capability Building (entries where analytical work built or materially improved a data process, even without strategic ownership)
- Capability: Data & Analytics; Context: Greenfield OR Scaling

**Medium priority (include where space permits):**

- Capability: Data & Analytics AND Capability: Process Design & Optimization (analytical work that directly produced a more reliable or efficient data process)
- Capability: Data & Analytics AND Capability: Quality & Compliance (regulatory data compliance, submission readiness, inspection-relevant data quality)
- Capability: Data & Analytics AND Capability: Risk-Based Monitoring & Quality (risk-based data review, signal detection, targeted monitoring analytical work)

**Use if space permits:**

- Capability: Data & Analytics AND Capability: Technology Implementation (only when the technology was a data platform or analytical tool and the contribution was in using or evaluating it, not governing it strategically)
- Capability: Stakeholder Management & Influence (only if analytical work directly informed a decision by a study team, functional lead, or external stakeholder)

**Avoid:**

- Capability: Governance & Risk Management without a Data & Analytics tag (route to Process & Operations unless data quality or analytics governance contribution)
- Organizational design or capability ownership language in achievement framing
- Summary references to data strategy ownership or enterprise governance

---

## Achievement framing language

Pattern for this archetype at IC level: data problem or analytical need; technical approach or solution; outcome or impact. Technical execution detail is appropriate at IC level. Methods, tools, and approaches should be included when they explain how the outcome was achievable. The outcome must still be stated in concrete impact terms (what improved, what risk reduced, what decision enabled), not just what was built.

---

## Calibration examples

**WEAK** (describes activity, no outcome):

> "Developed R scripts to analyze laboratory data submission trends and created dashboards for operational reporting."

**STRONG** (data problem first, technical approach, concrete outcome):

> "Identified a data stability risk in longitudinally evolving datasets not captured by point-in-time freeze controls, designed a continuous surveillance approach in R, and reduced rework burden on study teams across multiple programs."

**REFRAME** (same analytical work, outcome-focused):

> "Applied risk-based data quality assessment methods to external laboratory submissions, replacing manual review with structured analytics that reduced a near-50% conformance failure rate to zero within two submission cycles."

## Technical Proficiencies content

Specific content for the Technical Proficiencies section of a Clinical Development Data & Analytics IC CV (handled per the archetype skeleton's Technical Credibility pattern, with IC-level weight per content_rules_ic.md):

- Languages: R, Python, SQL, SAS, VBA
- ML frameworks: Scikit-learn, TensorFlow, Keras, NLTK
- Analytics environments: Jupyter, R Studio, Databricks, AWS
- Visualization tools: Spotfire, Power BI, Tableau

List fully and accurately per source documents. Do not compress or minimize at IC level.

---

## Content to de-emphasize

- Data strategy and governance ownership language: do not imply the candidate owned the analytics strategy, defined enterprise data standards, or directed governance frameworks
- Summary references to analytics capability leadership or data operating model design
- Organizational scope inflation (cross-functional governance authority, executive data decision influence, enterprise-level accountability) not supported by source documents
