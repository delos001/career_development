# Clinical Development: Archetype 4 Platform & Technology (Leadership)

Pack content for Archetype 4 at Leadership level within the Clinical Development domain. Load alongside the archetype skeleton at `rules/archetypes/Archetype_4_Platform_Technology.md` and level rules at `rules/cv/content_rules_leadership.md`.

---

## Match criteria

The role must center on one or more of:

- Technology strategy for clinical or data platforms: defining direction, evaluating options, and aligning the organization to a technology path
- Platform implementation leadership: owning or directing deployment of enterprise clinical systems, data platforms, or technology ecosystems
- System integration and interoperability: governing how clinical, data, and operational systems connect, exchange data, and maintain integrity across a technology ecosystem
- Vendor and tool ecosystem management: strategic oversight of external technology partners, platform selections, and build-versus-buy decisions at enterprise scale
- Technology operating model design: defining how technology capabilities are governed, supported, and evolved within a regulated clinical organization

Critical distinction within Clinical Development: this archetype is for roles where technology is the primary subject (platform, system, vendor ecosystem, or technology strategy). It is not correct for roles where technology is a supporting tool for data governance (Data & Analytics) or process improvement (Process & Operations). Verify against Transformation & Strategy if the role is primarily about driving organizational adoption of a technology change.

---

## Summary framing

- **Lead with:** technology strategy, platform leadership, and system governance in regulated clinical environments; not technical execution, hands-on implementation, or analytical capabilities
- **Do NOT lead with:** programming languages, data science methods, or operational process work
- **Register:** strategic leader who defines technology direction, governs platform ecosystems, and drives organizational alignment to technology decisions in regulated clinical development; not an IT implementer or clinical systems administrator
- **Required-element content:** years of experience in clinical technology environments (regulated pharma/biotech, sponsor or CRO); core capability signal (technology strategy, platform governance, clinical systems leadership, vendor ecosystem management, or build-versus-buy decision authority); evidence of strategic or organizational-level technology influence (enterprise platform selections, cross-functional technology governance, executive-level technology advisory); forward-looking statement connecting technology strategy experience to the platform challenge, modernization need, or ecosystem complexity implied by the job

## Inventory tag priorities

All queries for this archetype use compound tag combinations per the archetype skeleton's Compound Query Requirement. Single-tag queries on Technology Implementation alone return too many non-strategic entries.

**High priority (must be represented; flag if fewer than two coverable):**

- Capability: Technology Implementation AND Capability: Strategic Planning & Roadmapping; Role Level: Senior Manager, Director, or Senior Director (intersection of technology work and strategic direction-setting)
- Capability: Technology Implementation AND Capability: Governance & Risk Management; Outcome: Capability Building OR Scalability/Growth Enablement (governed technology decisions with organizational impact)
- Capability: Technology Implementation AND Capability: Data & Analytics; Context: Scaling OR Mature/Enterprise; Role Level: Director or Senior Director (platform decisions fundamentally driven by data strategy requirements)

**Medium priority (include where space permits):**

- Capability: Technology Implementation AND Capability: Stakeholder Management & Influence; Role Level: Director or Senior Director (cross-functional technology alignment, executive-level technology advisory)
- Capability: Strategic Planning & Roadmapping AND Capability: Technology Implementation; Context: Greenfield (technology strategy in a build-from-scratch context)
- Capability: Technology Implementation AND Capability: Change Management & Adoption (governing technology adoption at organizational scale, not individual user training)

**Use if space permits:**

- Capability: Process Design & Optimization AND Capability: Technology Implementation (only when the process was directly enabled by a platform or system decision; frame around the technology governance outcome)
- Capability: Organizational Design AND Capability: Technology Implementation (how a technology decision shaped or required an organizational capability or role structure)

**Avoid:**

- Capability: Technology Implementation at IC or Manager level without strategic or governance framing (hands-on system configuration, EDC setup, tool deployment without organizational technology decision authority)
- Capability: Process Design & Optimization without a Technology Implementation tag (route to Process & Operations)
- Capability: Data & Analytics without a Technology Implementation tag (route to Data & Analytics)
- Clinical trial execution achievements (trial management, site oversight, CRO management): earlier career context only

## Achievement framing language

Pattern for this archetype at Leadership level: technology risk, platform limitation, or ecosystem complexity; strategy decision or governance solution; organizational or capability outcome. The technology problem or strategic need must come first. Do not lead with the technology solution, the vendor selected, or the system deployed. The outcome must be stated in organizational terms (risk reduced, capability enabled, scale achieved), not technical performance terms.

### Domain knowledge as a differentiator

For Platform & Technology roles in clinical pharma/biotech, deep domain knowledge of the clinical systems landscape is a genuine differentiator that many pure technology leaders lack. Apply these rules to surface it appropriately:

- The Clinical Systems portion of Technical Proficiencies carries more weight here than in any other archetype. Ensure it is complete and current.
- Achievements that demonstrate understanding of how clinical data flows across a technology ecosystem (EDC through data management through submission) signal domain depth valuable for clinical platform roles. Surface this where present in the inventory.
- Advisory or consulting experience at a technology vendor's product level (bringing clinical domain knowledge into technology design) is relevant and belongs in the main experience section, not Earlier Professional Roles.

## Calibration examples

**WEAK** (describes technical implementation without strategic or governance framing):

> "Configured RAVE EDC system for new clinical studies, coordinating with data management and clinical operations to ensure system setup met protocol requirements and data collection standards."

**STRONG** (technology risk first, strategic decision, organizational outcome):

> "Identified that legacy macro-driven data processes could not scale with portfolio growth and carried increasing regulatory risk as complexity grew. Led cross-functional platform evaluation balancing change management impact against long-term scalability, driving the enterprise selection of Databricks to replace legacy infrastructure."

**REFRAME** (same advisory work repositioned as technology strategy leadership):

> "Brought clinical operations domain expertise into product design cycles for an RBM technology platform, translating field-level workflow requirements into platform features and advising developers on operational implications of design decisions. Ensured the resulting tool was grounded in field-level monitoring practice rather than abstract design assumptions."

## Technical Proficiencies content

Specific content for the Technical Proficiencies section of a Clinical Development Platform & Technology leadership CV (handled per the archetype skeleton's Section 6):

- Clinical Systems (priority subsection): EDC platforms, CTMS, IXRS/IRT, RBM tools, document management, third-party data systems, modern data platforms
- Languages (brief): R, Python, SQL, SAS
- Environments and platforms: Databricks, AWS, Git (current platform relevance; omit academic or legacy environments)
- ML and analytics frameworks (list level only): Scikit-learn, TensorFlow, Keras
- Visualization tools: Spotfire, Power BI, Tableau

List format only. Exclude proficiency level descriptors, first-appearance dates, and application narratives. Section remains last in the document.

---

## Content to de-emphasize

- Hands-on clinical operations and trial management work: site oversight, CRO management, trial execution; earlier career context only
- Pure data science or analytical achievements without technology strategy or platform governance framing (route to Data & Analytics)
- Process optimization without a technology dimension (route to Process & Operations)
- Summary references to data science credentials or analytical methods
- Tool configuration and system administration at hands-on level: EDC configuration, report building, script development are earlier career signals and must not appear in main experience blocks without strategic or governance reframing
