# Clinical Development: Archetype 1 Transformation & Strategy (Leadership)

Pack content for Archetype 1 at Leadership level within the Clinical Development domain. Load alongside the archetype skeleton at `rules/archetypes/Archetype_1_Transformation_Strategy.md` and level rules at `rules/cv/content_rules_leadership.md`.

---

## Match criteria

The role must center on one or more of:

- Enterprise-level organizational change, capability building from greenfield to scaled adoption, or operating model redesign
- Large-scale transformation programs requiring stakeholder navigation, cultural adoption, and resistance management
- Strategic program leadership where the primary deliverable is an organizational capability or structural change
- Change management at enterprise scope: multi-function, multi-region, or multi-program rollouts
- Digital or operational transformation with accountability for adoption outcomes, not just deployment

Verify against Process & Operations if the role centers on standardization or steady-state efficiency. Verify against Platform & Technology if the role centers on platform ownership or vendor ecosystem strategy. Transformation roles have greenfield or turnaround contexts at their core; if the role is primarily maintaining or optimizing an existing operating model, this is likely not the right archetype.

---

## Summary framing

- **Lead with:** organizational transformation experience, capability building at scale, and enterprise change leadership in regulated clinical environments
- **Do NOT lead with:** process optimization, technical capabilities, data science credentials, or steady-state operational management
- **Register:** leader who builds new organizational capabilities from greenfield, navigates complex stakeholder environments, and drives cultural adoption at scale; not an optimizer of existing operations
- **Required-element content:** years of experience in regulated clinical environments (pharma/biotech, global or cross-functional scale); core capability signal (enterprise transformation, greenfield capability building, change management at scale, or operating model redesign); evidence of complexity (stakeholder resistance, cultural shift, multi-function or multi-region scope, organizational ambiguity); forward-looking statement connecting transformation track record to the job's stated or implied organizational challenge

## Inventory tag priorities

**High priority (must be represented; flag if fewer than two coverable):**

- Capability: Change Management & Adoption; Context: Greenfield OR Scaling; Role Level: Senior Manager, Director, or Senior Director
- Capability: Organizational Design; Outcome: Capability Building; Context: Greenfield OR Turnaround
- Capability: Stakeholder Management & Influence; Context: Scaling OR Turnaround; Role Level: Director or Senior Director
- Outcome: Capability Building; Context: Greenfield (at least one achievement must show building from scratch)

**Medium priority (include where space permits):**

- Capability: Strategic Planning & Roadmapping (only when the roadmap is for an organizational capability or operating model, not a technology platform or process framework)
- Capability: Process Design & Optimization (Context: Greenfield only; use for new capability construction, not steady-state process improvement)
- Capability: Team Leadership & Development (only for building a new team or organizational capability, not managing an existing team)

**Use if space permits:**

- Capability: Governance & Risk Management (only when the governance structure was itself a new organizational capability being stood up)
- Capability: Financial Management & Budgeting (only for resource orchestration at transformation scale)

**Avoid:**

- Context: Mature/Enterprise without Greenfield or Turnaround
- Capability: Technology Implementation without a change management or adoption framing
- IC-level achievements without organizational scope
- Outcome: Efficiency Gain or Risk Reduction as primary outcomes

## Achievement framing language

Pattern for this archetype at Leadership level: organizational challenge or transformation need, change strategy and stakeholder navigation, adoption or capability outcome at scale. The organizational challenge must come first; do not lead with the solution or output artifact. The outcome must describe adoption, organizational capability, or structural change, not process efficiency or technical performance.

**Transformation Achievement Elements** (apply to the two or three most prominent achievements; compress all four into a 2-line bullet, do not expand into separate sentences):

- **Challenge:** the organizational problem, growth pressure, or capability gap that triggered the transformation
- **Strategy:** the change management approach, coalition, or phased design used
- **Execution:** how stakeholder resistance was navigated or what enabled adoption
- **Outcome:** the sustained organizational capability or scale metric that confirms adoption

## Calibration examples

**WEAK** (describes a process improvement, not a transformation):

> "Improved existing data acquisition process through workflow updates and team training sessions, resulting in improved data quality metrics."

**STRONG** (organizational challenge, change strategy, adoption at scale):

> "Built a governed data acquisition capability from greenfield as portfolio scale outpaced existing decentralized execution, securing adoption across eight global study teams through structured change management. Achieved consistent right-first-time execution within three submission cycles."

**REFRAME** (same work repositioned from operational to transformation framing):

> "Transitioned data acquisition from study-team-level execution to a governed enterprise capability as portfolio complexity grew, navigating resistance across five functional areas with no disruption to active submission timelines."

---

## Content to de-emphasize

- Process optimization language in the summary or leading achievements (route efficiency and standardization framing to the Process & Operations archetype)
- Technical skills section: include but keep brief at end; no elaboration on tools or methods
- Analytical and data science work: include only if it directly informed a transformation decision or enabled organizational change; do not present as standalone capability
- Steady-state management achievements: managing an ongoing operation is not transformation; exclude if no greenfield, scaling, or turnaround context
- Incremental improvements framed as transformation: resist language like "transformed the reporting process" for work that was fundamentally an optimization
