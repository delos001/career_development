# Clinical Development: Archetype 3 Process & Operations (Leadership)

Pack content for Archetype 3 at Leadership level within the Clinical Development domain. Load alongside the archetype skeleton at `rules/archetypes/Archetype_3_Process_Operations.md` and level rules at `rules/cv/content_rules_leadership.md`.

---

## Match criteria

The role must center on one or more of:

- Process design, standardization, or harmonization across clinical or regulated environments
- Operational efficiency, Lean/Six Sigma, or continuous improvement leadership
- Framework development, SOP governance, inspection readiness, or controlled documentation
- Cross-functional process ownership with risk management or quality systems emphasis
- CRO and vendor oversight models and external interface process management

Verify archetype assignment before applying if the role centers primarily on technology platform ownership (Platform & Technology), data strategy (Data & Analytics), or organizational transformation (Transformation & Strategy).

---

## Summary framing

- **Lead with:** process and governance expertise, cross-functional influence, and operational impact in regulated clinical environments
- **Do NOT lead with:** technical capabilities, data science credentials, programming languages, or analytical tools
- **Register:** strategic leader who builds scalable processes and governance structures; not a technical executor who optimizes workflows
- **Required-element content:** years of experience in regulated clinical environments (pharma/biotech sponsor or CRO); core capability signal (process design, governance, operational excellence, or inspection readiness); evidence of scale or organizational impact (global, cross-functional, enterprise, or multi-program scope); forward-looking statement connecting capability to the process or governance need implied by the job

---

## Inventory tag priorities

**High priority (must be represented; flag if fewer than two coverable):**

- Capability: Process Design & Optimization; Role Level: Senior Manager, Director, or Senior Director
- Capability: Governance & Risk Management; Context: Mature/Enterprise or Scaling
- Capability: Stakeholder Management & Influence; Role Level: Senior Manager, Director, or Senior Director
- Outcome: Risk Reduction OR Outcome: Efficiency Gain (at least one must appear with a metric or organizational result)

**Medium priority (include where space permits):**

- Capability: Strategic Planning & Roadmapping (only when the roadmap subject is process- or governance-focused, not technology-focused)
- Capability: Change Management & Adoption; Context: Scaling or Greenfield (process adoption at scale, not organizational transformation)
- Outcome: Quality Improvement (only if tied to a framework, governance structure, or SOP; not applicable for data analysis outcomes)

**Use if space permits:**

- Capability: Data & Analytics (only if the achievement produces a process or governance outcome; analytical work must be in service of the process, not the end product)
- Capability: Organizational Design (only if tied to process ownership structure, role clarity in a process context, or decision rights definition)

**Avoid:**

- Capability: Technology Implementation (exclude unless technology directly enabled a process or governance capability; if included, achievement must still lead with the process outcome)
- IC-level achievements without a process or framework outcome
- Purely analytical achievements without operational or organizational impact
- Training delivery unless it was enablement for a new capability designed and built

---

## Achievement framing language

Pattern for this archetype at Leadership level: business problem or operational risk; process/framework/governance solution; measurable or organizational outcome. The business problem must come first. Do not lead with the solution or with technical execution. The outcome must be organizational or measurable, not a description of an artifact produced.

---

## Calibration examples

**WEAK** (leads with technical execution, no process or governance framing):

> "Developed R scripts to analyze laboratory data submission trends and created dashboards for operational reporting."

**STRONG** (business problem first, process solution, organizational outcome):

> "Eliminated a near-50% rework rate on external laboratory submissions by redesigning acquisition procedures, clarifying decision rights across three functional areas, and embedding governance oversight into downstream processes. Reduced rework to zero within two submission cycles."

**REFRAME** (same underlying work, repositioned for this archetype):

> "Established data-driven quality surveillance for external laboratory submissions, designing analytics frameworks and operational reporting that enabled proactive identification of conformance risks and reduced submission rework. Replaced a reactive oversight model with a governed, repeatable operating capability."

## Content to de-emphasize

- Technical skills section: keep short; list only, no proficiency levels or applications
- Programming languages: comma-separated items only; no descriptions, dates, or applications
- Machine learning and modeling work: omit unless it directly produced a process or governance capability; if included, lead with the process outcome
- Data science framing in the summary (route to Data & Analytics archetype)
- Tool-specific achievements (route to Platform & Technology archetype)
