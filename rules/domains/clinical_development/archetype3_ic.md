# Clinical Development: Archetype 3 Process & Operations (IC)

Pack content for Archetype 3 at IC level within the Clinical Development domain. Load alongside the archetype skeleton at `rules/archetypes/Archetype_3_Process_Operations.md` and level rules at `rules/cv/content_rules_ic.md`.

---

## Match criteria

The role must center on one or more of:

- Individual process design, SOP development, or procedure writing in a regulated clinical environment
- Operational execution and quality: following, improving, or troubleshooting clinical or data management processes
- Lean/Six Sigma application at the project or functional level (process mapping, root cause analysis, waste reduction)
- Inspection readiness support: document preparation, controlled documentation, audit trail maintenance
- Clinical operations execution: site coordination, CRO interface management, trial-level operational problem solving

Verify against Data & Analytics IC if the role centers primarily on hands-on data analysis or data quality work without a process design component. Verify against Transformation & Strategy IC if the role centers on change program delivery. This archetype applies when the primary output is a process artifact, operational procedure, or execution quality improvement.

---

## Summary framing

- **Lead with:** process design expertise, operational problem-solving, and delivery in regulated clinical environments
- **Do NOT lead with:** cross-functional ownership, enterprise governance authority, or strategic process leadership
- **Register:** skilled practitioner who designs, implements, and improves processes and operating procedures in regulated environments; with the technical and domain depth to solve real operational problems
- **Required-element content:** years of experience in regulated clinical environments (pharma/biotech sponsor or CRO); core capability signal (process design, SOP development, operational efficiency, quality systems, or inspection readiness); evidence of scope or complexity (types of processes, functional areas engaged, operational problems addressed); forward-looking statement connecting process and operational experience to the need implied by the job

## Inventory tag priorities

**High priority (must be represented; flag if fewer than two coverable):**

- Capability: Process Design & Optimization; Outcome: Efficiency Gain OR Risk Reduction
- Capability: Process Design & Optimization; Outcome: Quality Improvement
- Capability: Quality & Compliance; Context: any (inspection readiness, SOP governance, controlled documentation)

**Medium priority (include where space permits):**

- Capability: Process Design & Optimization AND Capability: Change Management & Adoption (process improvements requiring active rollout or training)
- Capability: Clinical Trial Execution (operational execution depth and domain knowledge in trial context)
- Capability: Vendor Management & Oversight (process contributions involving external partner coordination or interface process design)

**Use if space permits:**

- Capability: Data & Analytics AND Capability: Process Design & Optimization (only when analytical work directly produced a process or operational outcome; frame around process result)
- Capability: Risk-Based Monitoring & Quality (monitoring process contributions, risk-based oversight procedures, quality signal identification)

**Avoid:**

- Capability: Technology Implementation without a process outcome (route to Platform & Technology unless primary output was a process enabled by the technology)
- Organizational design or enterprise governance language
- Summary references to strategic process leadership or cross-functional governance ownership not supported by source documents

---

## Achievement framing language

Pattern for this archetype at IC level: operational problem or process gap; specific process or framework designed or improved; measurable or organizational outcome. Technical execution detail is appropriate at IC level. The specific process, procedure, or framework built or improved should be named. The outcome must be stated in concrete terms (what improved, what risk reduced, what was standardized).

---

## Calibration examples

**WEAK** (describes output, no problem or outcome):

> "Developed R scripts to analyze laboratory data submission trends and created dashboards for operational reporting."

**STRONG** (operational problem first, specific solution, concrete outcome):

> "Redesigned external laboratory data acquisition and conformance procedures to address a near-50% submission rework rate, clarifying decision rights and embedding governance checkpoints into downstream processes. Rework reached zero within two submission cycles."

**REFRAME** (same work, process contribution and outcome clearly stated):

> "Designed structured analytics and operational reporting for external laboratory submission oversight, enabling proactive conformance risk identification. Replaced a reactive review model with a governed, repeatable operating approach."

---

## Content to de-emphasize

- Cross-functional ownership and enterprise governance language (do not imply operating model ownership, governance direction, or enterprise process authority)
- Summary references to strategic process leadership
- Organizational design signals (role clarity, decision rights, accountability model design): appropriate only if the specific contribution was the design work, not organizational ownership of the outcome
- Technical tools section: include with appropriate weight. Lean/Six Sigma tools, process modeling software, and clinical systems familiarity are relevant IC-level signals; list accurately
