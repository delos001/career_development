# Clinical Development: Archetype 1 Transformation & Strategy (IC)

Pack content for Archetype 1 at IC level within the Clinical Development domain. Load alongside the archetype skeleton at `rules/archetypes/Archetype_1_Transformation_Strategy.md` and level rules at `rules/cv/content_rules_ic.md`.

---

## Match criteria

The role must center on one or more of:

- Execution within a transformation program; delivering workstreams, coordinating adoption activities, or enabling change at the project or functional level
- Change enablement support: training materials, communication plans, stakeholder engagement assets, readiness assessments within a larger change initiative
- Cross-functional coordination in support of an operating model or capability-building effort; not ownership of the program
- Analytical or design contributions that inform transformation decisions: process mapping, gap analysis, current-state assessment, adoption tracking
- Program or project management within a transformation context; not strategic direction-setting

Verify against Process & Operations IC if the role is primarily individual process design or SOP development without a transformation or change program context. Verify against Platform & Technology IC if the role is primarily individual technology implementation or system configuration. This archetype applies when the role's primary context is a change or transformation initiative, even if the contribution is individual.

---

## Summary framing

- **Lead with:** transformation program experience, change enablement, and cross-functional execution in regulated clinical environments
- **Do NOT lead with:** organizational design authority, enterprise-level strategic direction, or capability-building ownership
- **Register:** skilled practitioner who delivers within transformation programs; executing workstreams, enabling adoption, and contributing analysis that informs transformation decisions
- **Required-element content:** years of experience in regulated clinical environments (pharma/biotech); core capability signal (change enablement, transformation program delivery, stakeholder engagement, or cross-functional coordination); evidence of contribution complexity (scope of work within a larger program, types of stakeholders engaged, analytical or design contributions made); forward-looking statement connecting transformation execution experience to the job's stated or implied program need

## Inventory tag priorities

**High priority (must be represented; flag if fewer than two coverable):**

- Capability: Change Management & Adoption; Outcome: Capability Building OR Scalability/Growth Enablement
- Capability: Stakeholder Management & Influence; Context: Greenfield OR Scaling
- Capability: Process Design & Optimization; Context: Greenfield OR Scaling (only where the process contribution was part of a transformation or change initiative)

**Medium priority (include where space permits):**

- Capability: Organizational Design (only if the contribution was a design input: mapping, analysis, recommendation; not ownership of the design decision)
- Capability: Strategic Planning & Roadmapping (only if analytical or coordination work informed the roadmap, not if it was owned)
- Capability: Team Leadership & Development (only if it shows enabling or onboarding others as part of a change program, not managing a team)

**Use if space permits:**

- Capability: Data & Analytics (only if the analytical work directly supported a transformation decision or adoption tracking)
- Capability: Technology Implementation (only if the technology was being adopted as part of a transformation and the contribution was change enablement, not technical implementation)

**Avoid:**

- Capability: Governance & Risk Management without a change or adoption context
- Steady-state operational or process maintenance work (Context: Mature/Enterprise without Greenfield, Scaling, or Turnaround signal)
- Outcome: Efficiency Gain as a primary outcome (route to Process & Operations unless the efficiency gain was a direct consequence of a transformation program)

## Achievement framing language

Pattern for this archetype at IC level: program need or change challenge, specific contribution or deliverable, outcome or impact on program success. The specific contribution comes from the candidate's own work, not the program outcome as a whole. Do not claim ownership of enterprise-level outcomes. Do state the impact of the contribution on the program, workstream, or team. Technical execution is appropriate and expected at this level.

---

## Calibration examples

**WEAK** (describes activity without impact):

> "Supported change management activities for a clinical data transformation initiative, including preparing training materials and attending stakeholder meetings."

**STRONG** (specific contribution, clear program impact):

> "Designed and delivered training infrastructure for a cross-functional data acquisition capability rollout, enabling consistent onboarding across eight study teams. Adoption reached full compliance within the first submission cycle."

**REFRAME** (same work, contribution framed with program impact):

> "Developed the stakeholder engagement plan and communication materials that supported a governed data acquisition transition across five functional areas, contributing to a change management approach that produced no disruption to active submission timelines."

---

## Content to de-emphasize

- Organizational ownership language: do not write achievements that imply ownership of the transformation, designing the change strategy, or directing organizational adoption; these are leadership-level signals that misrepresent IC contribution and create interview exposure
- Summary references to enterprise capability-building or operating model redesign
- Strategic direction language: avoid "defined strategy," "established direction," or "led enterprise adoption" unless explicitly accurate; replace with "contributed to," "supported," "delivered within," or "enabled"
