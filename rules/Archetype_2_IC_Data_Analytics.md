**ARCHETYPE 2 IC: DATA & ANALYTICS**

*CV Generation Instruction Set --- Jason Delosh*

**1. Purpose and Scope**

Apply this document when the archetype is Data & Analytics and the role level confirmed in Phase 2a is Individual Contributor. Use in combination with `rules/cv/content_rules_ic.md`, which governs voice, register, and formatting. Do not apply leadership framing from `Archetype_2_Data_Analytics.md` for IC roles.

**2. Archetype Match Criteria**

Confirm this is the correct archetype before proceeding. The role must center on one or more of the following:

-   Hands-on clinical data management, data quality review, or data cleaning in regulated environments

-   Individual analytical delivery --- building models, writing scripts, producing reports, or performing statistical analysis

-   Data science application --- applying ML, statistical, or computational methods to clinical or operational data problems

-   Data quality monitoring, issue identification, and root cause analysis at the study or program level

-   Regulatory data compliance --- CDISC standards, SDTM/ADaM mapping, submission readiness at the dataset or deliverable level

Critical distinction: this archetype applies when the role's primary output is analytical work, data deliverables, or data quality execution --- not data strategy or governance ownership. If the role is primarily about building or governing an analytics capability at organizational scale, it may be above IC level --- flag this in the fit assessment. If the role is primarily about technology platform configuration, verify against the Platform & Technology IC archetype.

**3. Required Section Order**

Build the CV in exactly this section order:

-   1\. Professional Summary

-   2\. Core Competencies

-   3\. Professional Experience

-   4\. Education

-   5\. Certifications and Training

-   6\. Technical Proficiencies (last section --- given more weight than in leadership CVs; expand to fully represent analytical tools, languages, and environments per `rules/cv/content_rules_ic.md`)

Core Competencies should list 8 to 10 items spanning two zones: (1) clinical data and analytical capabilities --- 5 to 6 items, (2) technical credibility --- 3 to 4 items. The technical zone is more prominent for this archetype than for others at IC level. Do not list data strategy, governance leadership, or analytics capability ownership --- these are leadership-level signals.

**4. Inventory Tag Priorities**

Query the Experience Inventory using these tag combinations in priority order. Do not filter by Role Level --- entries from any role level are eligible. The achievement framing rules in Section 3 govern how entries are presented at IC level.

**High Priority --- Must Be Represented**

These tag combinations must map to at least one achievement in the finished CV. If fewer than two are coverable from the inventory, flag the gap explicitly before generating.

-   Capability: Data & Analytics --- Outcome: Quality Improvement OR Risk Reduction

-   Capability: Data & Analytics --- Outcome: Capability Building --- use for entries where analytical work built or materially improved a data process, even if ownership was not at the strategic level

-   Capability: Data & Analytics --- Context: Greenfield OR Scaling --- prioritize entries that show analytical contribution in a build or growth environment

**Medium Priority --- Include Where Space Permits**

-   Capability: Data & Analytics AND Capability: Process Design & Optimization --- use when the analytical work directly produced a more reliable or efficient data process

-   Capability: Data & Analytics AND Capability: Quality & Compliance --- use for regulatory data compliance, submission readiness, or inspection-relevant data quality work

-   Capability: Data & Analytics AND Capability: Risk-Based Monitoring & Quality --- use for risk-based data review, signal detection, or targeted monitoring analytical work

**Use If Space Permits**

-   Capability: Data & Analytics AND Capability: Technology Implementation --- use only when the technology was a data platform or analytical tool and Jason's contribution was in using or evaluating it, not governing it strategically

-   Capability: Stakeholder Management & Influence --- only if the analytical work directly informed a decision by a study team, functional lead, or external stakeholder

**Avoid for This Archetype**

-   Capability: Governance & Risk Management without a Data & Analytics tag --- pure governance work belongs in Process & Operations unless it was a data quality or analytics governance contribution

-   Organizational design or capability ownership language in achievement framing --- reframe to the specific analytical contribution

-   Summary references to data strategy ownership or enterprise governance --- route to the leadership archetype if those are accurate signals

**5. Summary Framing Rules**

**Lead with:** Clinical data expertise, analytical depth, and technical execution in regulated environments

**Do NOT lead with:** Data strategy ownership, enterprise governance authority, or analytics capability leadership

**Register:** Technically capable practitioner who delivers reliable, decision-relevant analytical work in regulated clinical environments --- grounded in both domain knowledge and technical execution

**Length:** 3 to 4 sentences only

Required elements --- all four must be present:

-   Years of experience and domain (clinical data, regulated environments, pharma/biotech)

-   Core capability signal --- one of: clinical data management, data quality, analytical delivery, data science application, or regulatory data compliance

-   Evidence of technical or domain depth: specific methods, environments, or problem types handled

-   One forward-looking statement connecting Jason's analytical and clinical data experience to the specific technical need stated or implied in the job description

**3. Achievement Framing Rules**

Every achievement must follow this structure:

Data problem or analytical need -\> Technical approach or solution -\> Outcome or impact

Technical execution detail is appropriate at IC level. Methods, tools, and approaches should be included when they explain how the outcome was achievable. The outcome must still be stated in concrete impact terms --- what improved, what risk was reduced, what decision was enabled --- not just what was built.

Every achievement must be expressed within 2 lines. 3 lines is acceptable only for the highest-complexity achievements where technical or outcome detail cannot be compressed further. 4 or more lines is never acceptable.

**Calibration Examples**

**WEAK --- describes activity, no outcome:**

*\"Developed R scripts to analyze laboratory data submission trends and created dashboards for operational reporting.\"*

**STRONG --- data problem first, technical approach, concrete outcome:**

*\"Identified a data stability risk in longitudinally evolving datasets not captured by point-in-time freeze controls, designed a continuous surveillance approach in R, and reduced rework burden on study teams across multiple programs.\"*

**REFRAME --- same analytical work, outcome-focused:**

*\"Applied risk-based data quality assessment methods to external laboratory submissions, replacing manual review with structured analytics that reduced a near-50% conformance failure rate to zero within two submission cycles.\"*

**7. Content to De-emphasize**

-   Data strategy and governance ownership language --- do not write achievements that imply Jason owned the analytics strategy, defined enterprise data standards, or directed governance frameworks. Reframe to the specific analytical contribution and its impact.

-   Summary references to analytics capability leadership or data operating model design --- the summary positions Jason as a technically capable practitioner, not a strategic data leader.

-   Organizational scope inflation --- do not imply cross-functional governance authority, executive data decision influence, or enterprise-level accountability not present in source documents.

-   Technical tools section: expand this section relative to leadership CVs. Languages, frameworks, environments, and analytical tools are prominent signals at IC level --- list fully and accurately per source documents. Do not compress or minimize.
