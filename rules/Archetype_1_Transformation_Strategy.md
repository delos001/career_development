**ARCHETYPE 1: TRANSFORMATION & STRATEGY**

*CV Generation Instruction Set --- Jason Delosh*

**1. Purpose and Scope**

This document is an instruction set for Phase 4a CV content generation. Apply all rules in this document when the archetype identified in Phase 2a is Transformation & Strategy. Do not apply these rules for any other archetype. If a dual-archetype was identified in Phase 2a, apply primary framing from this document and secondary framing selectively within individual achievements only where both dimensions are genuinely present in the underlying experience.

**2. Archetype Match Criteria**

Confirm this is the correct archetype before proceeding. The role must center on one or more of the following:

-   Enterprise-level organizational change, capability building from greenfield to scaled adoption, or operating model redesign

-   Large-scale transformation programs requiring stakeholder navigation, cultural adoption, and resistance management

-   Strategic program leadership where the primary deliverable is an organizational capability or structural change --- not a product, process, or technology

-   Change management at enterprise scope: multi-function, multi-region, or multi-program rollouts

-   Digital or operational transformation with accountability for adoption outcomes, not just deployment

If the role centers primarily on process standardization and steady-state operational efficiency, verify against the Process & Operations archetype. If it centers on technology platform ownership or vendor ecosystem strategy, verify against the Platform & Technology archetype. Transformation roles have greenfield or turnaround contexts at their core --- if the role is primarily maintaining or optimizing an existing operating model, this is likely not the right archetype.

**3. Summary Framing Rules**

Write the professional summary using all of the following rules without exception:

**Lead with:** Organizational transformation experience, capability building at scale, and enterprise change leadership in regulated clinical environments

**Do NOT lead with:** Process optimization, technical capabilities, data science credentials, or steady-state operational management

**Register:** Leader who builds new organizational capabilities from greenfield, navigates complex stakeholder environments, and drives cultural adoption at scale --- not an optimizer of existing operations

**Length:** 3 to 4 sentences only

Required elements --- all four must be present:

-   Years of experience and domain (regulated clinical environments, pharma/biotech, global or cross-functional scale)

-   Core capability signal --- one of: enterprise transformation, greenfield capability building, change management at scale, or operating model redesign

-   Evidence of complexity navigated: stakeholder resistance, cultural shift, multi-function or multi-region scope, or organizational ambiguity

-   One forward-looking statement connecting Jason\'s transformation track record to the specific organizational challenge stated or implied in the job description

**4. Required Section Order**

Build the CV in exactly this section order:

-   1\. Professional Summary

-   2\. Core Competencies

-   3\. Professional Experience

-   4\. Selected Projects (include when entries exist in the Independent & Volunteer Projects inventory section; omit if section is empty)

-   5\. Education

-   6\. Certifications and Training

-   7\. Technical Proficiencies (last section --- condensed, minimal visual weight)

Core Competencies should list 8 to 12 items and prioritize transformation, change management, organizational design, and capability-building capabilities. Operational and technical capabilities appear only if they directly supported a transformation outcome and only if space permits.

**5. Inventory Tag Priorities**

Query the Experience Inventory using these tag combinations in priority order. Each tier defines the minimum representation required in the finished CV.

**High Priority --- Must Be Represented**

These tag combinations must map to at least one achievement in the finished CV. If fewer than two are coverable from the inventory, flag the gap explicitly before generating.

-   Capability: Change Management & Adoption --- Context: Greenfield OR Scaling --- Role Level: Senior Manager, Director, or Senior Director

-   Capability: Organizational Design --- Outcome: Capability Building --- Context: Greenfield OR Turnaround

-   Capability: Stakeholder Management & Influence --- Context: Scaling OR Turnaround --- Role Level: Director or Senior Director

-   Outcome: Capability Building --- Context: Greenfield (at least one achievement must show building from scratch, not improving an existing capability)

**Medium Priority --- Include Where Space Permits**

-   Capability: Strategic Planning & Roadmapping --- only when the roadmap is for an organizational capability or operating model, not a technology platform or process framework

-   Capability: Process Design & Optimization --- Context: Greenfield only. Use to show new capability construction, not steady-state process improvement. Do not use Mature/Enterprise context entries for this archetype.

-   Capability: Team Leadership & Development --- only if it shows building a new team or organizational capability from scratch, not managing an existing team

**Use If Space Permits**

-   Capability: Governance & Risk Management --- only if the governance structure was itself a new organizational capability being stood up, not an incremental compliance improvement

-   Capability: Financial Management & Budgeting --- only if it demonstrates resource orchestration at transformation scale (securing budget for a greenfield capability, not managing a steady-state budget)

**Avoid for This Archetype**

-   Context: Mature/Enterprise without Greenfield or Turnaround --- steady-state operational achievements do not belong in a transformation CV

-   Capability: Technology Implementation without a change management or adoption framing --- technology deployment without organizational adoption is Platform & Technology, not Transformation

-   IC-level achievements without organizational scope --- individual technical or analytical work is not relevant here

-   Outcome: Efficiency Gain or Risk Reduction as primary outcomes --- these are Process & Operations outcomes. Use only if they were consequences of a transformation, not the transformation itself

**6. Achievement Selection and Framing Rules**

**Selection Criteria**

Select an achievement only if it satisfies all three criteria:

-   Describes a new organizational capability, operating model, or structural change --- not a process improvement or technology deployment

-   States or clearly implies the organizational challenge, resistance, or ambiguity that made the transformation non-trivial

-   Includes an adoption or scale outcome: number of users, teams, regions, or programs affected; adoption rate; or time-to-scale

**Required Framing Pattern**

Every achievement must follow this structure:

Organizational challenge or transformation need -\> Change strategy and stakeholder navigation -\> Adoption or capability outcome at scale

The organizational challenge must come first. Do not lead with the solution or the output artifact. The outcome must describe adoption, organizational capability, or structural change --- not process efficiency or technical performance. Every achievement must be expressed within 2 lines. 3 lines is acceptable only for the highest-complexity achievements where scope or outcome detail cannot be compressed further. 4 or more lines is never acceptable.

**Transformation Achievement Elements --- Apply to Most Prominent Achievements**

For the two or three most prominent achievements in the CV, ensure all four elements below are present within the bullet. This is not required for every achievement but must appear at least twice in the BioMarin experience block. These are content requirements, not a sequence of sentences --- compress all four into a 2-line bullet. Do not expand each element into its own sentence. Do not write a multi-sentence narrative. The goal is a single precise bullet that signals all four dimensions:

-   Challenge: The organizational problem, growth pressure, or capability gap that triggered the transformation

-   Strategy: The change management approach, coalition, or phased design used

-   Execution: How stakeholder resistance was navigated or what enabled adoption

-   Outcome: The sustained organizational capability or scale metric that confirms adoption

**Calibration Examples**

**WEAK --- describes a process improvement, not a transformation:**

*\"Improved existing data acquisition process through workflow updates and team training sessions, resulting in improved data quality metrics.\"*

**STRONG --- organizational challenge, change strategy, adoption at scale:**

*\"Built a governed data acquisition capability from greenfield as portfolio scale outpaced existing decentralized execution, securing adoption across eight global study teams through structured change management. Achieved consistent right-first-time execution within three submission cycles.\"*

**REFRAME --- same work repositioned from operational to transformation framing:**

*\"Transitioned data acquisition from study-team-level execution to a governed enterprise capability as portfolio complexity grew, navigating resistance across five functional areas with no disruption to active submission timelines.\"*

**7. Content to De-emphasize**

-   Process optimization language in the summary or leading achievements --- route efficiency and standardization framing to the Process & Operations archetype

-   Technical skills section: include but keep to a brief list at the end. No elaboration on tools or methods.

-   Analytical and data science work: include only if it directly informed a transformation decision or enabled organizational change. Do not present it as a standalone capability.

-   Steady-state management achievements: managing an ongoing operation is not transformation. If an achievement does not have a greenfield, scaling, or turnaround context, exclude it or reframe if genuinely applicable.

-   Incremental improvements framed as transformation: resist language like \'transformed the reporting process\' for work that was fundamentally an optimization. Recruiters and hiring managers at this level recognize the distinction.
