**ARCHETYPE 2: DATA & ANALYTICS**

*CV Generation Instruction Set --- Jason Delosh*

**1. Purpose and Scope**

This document is an instruction set for CV content generation. Apply all rules in this document when the confirmed archetype is Data & Analytics. Do not apply these rules for any other archetype. If a dual-archetype was identified, apply primary framing from this document and secondary framing selectively within individual achievements only where both dimensions are genuinely present in the underlying experience.

**2. Archetype Match Criteria**

Confirm this is the correct archetype before proceeding. The role must center on one or more of the following:

-   Clinical data strategy --- defining how data is acquired, governed, assessed for quality, and made decision-ready across a portfolio or organization

-   Data governance --- establishing standards, ownership structures, accountability models, and quality frameworks for clinical or operational data

-   Analytics capability development --- building or maturing an organization\'s ability to generate decision-relevant insight from clinical or operational data

-   Data science enablement --- establishing the infrastructure, processes, or governance that allows data science to operate reliably at scale in a regulated environment

-   Analytics operating model design --- defining how analytics functions are organized, resourced, governed, and measured

Critical distinction: this archetype is for roles where the primary output is a data or analytics capability --- a governed system, a decision-relevant process, a mature operating model. It is not the correct archetype for roles primarily about building or owning a technology platform (Platform & Technology), nor for roles primarily about process standardization where data is incidental (Process & Operations). If the role is for a hands-on individual contributor analytics or data science role, it falls below Jason\'s target level and should be flagged in the fit assessment before proceeding to CV generation.

**3. Summary Framing Rules**

Write the professional summary using all of the following rules without exception:

**Lead with:** Data strategy, governance, and analytics capability leadership in regulated clinical environments --- not technical execution or individual analytical output

**Do NOT lead with:** Programming languages, machine learning methods, specific tools, or hands-on data science work. Jason\'s MS in Data Science is a credential signal, not the lead.

**Register:** Strategic data leader who builds decision-relevant, governed analytics capabilities at enterprise or portfolio scale --- not an analyst who produces models and reports

**Length:** 3 to 4 sentences only

Required elements --- all four must be present:

-   Years of experience and domain (clinical data, regulated environments, pharma/biotech sponsor or CRO)

-   Core capability signal --- one of: data strategy, analytics governance, data quality leadership, analytics capability maturity, or clinical data operating model

-   Evidence of organizational or portfolio-scale impact: cross-functional governance, enterprise standards, multi-program data ecosystems, or executive-level influence on data decisions

-   One forward-looking statement connecting Jason\'s data strategy and governance track record to the specific analytical challenge or maturity need stated or implied in the job description

**4. Required Section Order**

Build the CV in exactly this section order:

-   1\. Professional Summary

-   2\. Core Competencies

-   3\. Professional Experience

-   4\. Selected Projects (include when entries exist in the Independent & Volunteer Projects inventory section; omit if section is empty)

-   5\. Education

-   6\. Certifications and Training

-   7\. Technical Proficiencies (positioned last but given more weight than in other archetypes --- see Section 7)

Core Competencies should list 8 to 12 items. For this archetype, competencies should span three zones: (1) data strategy and governance --- 4 to 5 items, (2) analytics capability and operating model --- 3 to 4 items, (3) technical credibility --- 2 to 3 items. The technical zone confirms hands-on literacy without positioning Jason as a practitioner. Do not allow the technical zone to dominate.

**5. Inventory Tag Priorities**

Query the Experience Inventory using these tag combinations in priority order. Each tier defines the minimum representation required in the finished CV.

**High Priority --- Must Be Represented**

These tag combinations must map to at least one achievement in the finished CV. If fewer than two are coverable from the inventory, flag the gap explicitly before generating.

-   Capability: Data & Analytics --- Role Level: Senior Manager, Director, or Senior Director --- Outcome: Capability Building

-   Capability: Governance & Risk Management --- Capability: Data & Analytics --- Outcome: Quality Improvement OR Capability Building

-   Capability: Strategic Planning & Roadmapping --- Capability: Data & Analytics --- Context: Scaling OR Mature/Enterprise

-   Outcome: Capability Building --- Context: Greenfield OR Scaling --- at least one achievement must show building or materially maturing an analytics capability, not maintaining one

**Medium Priority --- Include Where Space Permits**

-   Capability: Organizational Design --- Capability: Data & Analytics --- use to show how analytics functions were structured, roles defined, or accountability models established

-   Capability: Process Design & Optimization --- Capability: Data & Analytics --- use only when the process outcome directly governed how data was assessed, qualified, or made decision-ready. Do not use for general process improvements where data was incidental.

-   Capability: Stakeholder Management & Influence --- Role Level: Director or Senior Director --- use to demonstrate executive influence on data strategy or governance decisions

**Use If Space Permits**

-   Capability: Technology Implementation --- Capability: Data & Analytics --- use only when the technology decision was fundamentally a data strategy decision (e.g., platform selection driven by data governance requirements). The achievement must still lead with the data or governance outcome.

-   Capability: Change Management & Adoption --- use only when adoption of a new data standard, governance model, or analytics operating practice required active change management at scale

**Avoid for This Archetype**

-   Hands-on analytical work presented as a standalone achievement --- individual model builds, script development, or dashboard creation without an organizational or governance outcome belong in earlier career context only, not in recent roles

-   Technical tool achievements without data strategy or governance framing --- implementing a visualization tool or writing SQL is not a Data & Analytics leadership achievement

-   IC-level data science work in BioMarin, AbbVie, or PRA roles --- these must be reframed at the appropriate governance and strategy level or excluded

-   Capability: Process Design & Optimization without a Data & Analytics tag --- pure process achievements belong in the Process & Operations archetype

**6. Achievement Selection and Framing Rules**

**Selection Criteria**

Select an achievement only if it satisfies all three criteria:

-   Describes a data governance structure, analytics operating model, data quality framework, or decision-relevant capability --- not an analytical output, model, or report

-   States or clearly implies the data quality risk, governance gap, or analytical maturity problem that drove the work

-   Includes an organizational scope signal: cross-functional data standards, enterprise governance, multi-program data ecosystems, or executive-level data decision influence

**Required Framing Pattern**

Every achievement must follow this structure:

Data quality risk, governance gap, or analytical maturity problem -\> Governance/strategy/capability solution -\> Decision-relevant or organizational outcome

The data problem must come first. Do not lead with the technical solution or the artifact produced. The outcome must be stated in terms of organizational impact --- what decisions became better, what risks were reduced, what scale was enabled --- not in terms of technical performance metrics. Every achievement must be expressed within 2 lines. 3 lines is acceptable only for the highest-complexity achievements where scope or outcome detail cannot be compressed further. 4 or more lines is never acceptable.

**Managing the Technical Credibility Signal**

This archetype has a specific tension that requires active management: Jason\'s technical depth (MS Data Science, R, Python, ML) is a genuine differentiator for Data & Analytics roles, but leading with it positions him as a practitioner rather than a strategic leader. Apply these rules to resolve the tension correctly:

-   Technical methods and tools appear in achievement descriptions only when they explain how the governance or strategy outcome was achievable --- not as the subject of the achievement itself

-   The MS in Data Science credential belongs in the Education section and can be referenced once in the summary as a credentialing signal. It should not drive achievement framing.

-   The Technical Proficiencies section for this archetype carries more weight than in other archetypes. Expand it modestly to confirm hands-on analytical literacy --- languages, environments, ML frameworks --- without elaborating on proficiency levels or specific applications. The section signals capability; the experience section demonstrates leadership.

-   If a role description emphasizes hands-on analytics delivery alongside strategic leadership, the technical skills section can be expanded by one paragraph. Do not expand beyond that without flagging it as a potential level mismatch in the fit assessment.

**Calibration Examples**

**WEAK --- leads with technical execution, no governance or strategy framing:**

*\"Developed R-based data transformation and aggregation procedures to enhance data review and compliance monitoring, investigating issue root causes and identifying data quality risks.\"*

**STRONG --- data quality problem first, governance solution, organizational outcome:**

*\"Redefined data quality planning across global clinical programs, replacing retrospective validation with proactive quality specifications and structured ownership models. Established a governance framework creating consistent accountability across study teams and enabling KPI-based oversight.\"*

**REFRAME --- same technical work repositioned as data strategy leadership:**

*\"Identified a systemic data stability risk in point-in-time freeze controls that could not detect meaningful change in longitudinally evolving datasets. Replaced the legacy model with a continuous risk-based surveillance capability that scaled across programs and reduced rework burden on study teams.\"*

**7. Technical Proficiencies Section --- Archetype-Specific Handling**

The Technical Proficiencies section for this archetype differs from the other three. Apply these rules instead of the default minimal treatment:

-   Include the full languages list: R, Python, SQL, SAS, VBA --- these signal practitioner-level literacy that is relevant for Data & Analytics leadership roles

-   Include ML frameworks: Scikit-learn, TensorFlow, Keras, NLTK --- these confirm that Jason can evaluate, direct, and govern data science work, not just commission it

-   Include analytics environments: Jupyter, R Studio, Databricks, AWS --- relevant context for platform-adjacent data strategy conversations

-   Include visualization tools: Spotfire, Power BI, Tableau --- relevant for analytics operating model discussions

-   Still exclude: proficiency level descriptors, first-appearance dates, and application narratives. List format only.

-   Still keep the section last in the document. Technical depth is a supporting credential, not the lead.

**8. Content to De-emphasize**

-   IC-level analytical work in recent roles --- any achievement from BioMarin or AbbVie that describes individual data analysis, script writing, or model building must be reframed at the governance and strategy level or excluded

-   Clinical operations management achievements --- trial execution, site management, and CRO oversight belong in earlier career context only for this archetype, unless they directly informed a data strategy or governance decision

-   Process standardization without data governance framing --- route pure process achievements to the Process & Operations archetype

-   Summary mentions of specific tools or languages --- the summary establishes strategic positioning. Tool references belong in Technical Proficiencies only.
