# Organization Type Registry

This file is the authoritative catalog of CV format references keyed to organization
type and maturity. Read this file when identifying the appropriate CV template for
the target company. Load the corresponding template file in Phase 4a as instructed
by the skill.

---

## Large Enterprise Established

**Use when:** The target company is an established pharma, large CRO, or mature
biotech. Indicators: stable operating model, scaled infrastructure, established
regulatory and governance posture.

**Do NOT use when:** The company is in a growth or transformation state -- size
alone does not determine this selection. A large company undergoing significant
transformation belongs in Mid-Size Scale-Up.

**Framing emphasis:** Archetype governs achievement selection and framing. This
guidance adjusts how the organizational environment and operating context are
described -- not how achievements are characterized.

The target organization has established governance, process infrastructure, and
regulatory maturity. Frame the operating context accordingly -- you are entering
an environment with institutional structure, not building one from the ground up.
Emphasize your ability to operate effectively within complex matrix structures,
drive cross-functional alignment, and deliver within established governance and
compliance frameworks. Outcomes should reflect rigor, durability, and
organizational fit. Where transformation or capability-building work is present,
frame it as addressing a specific gap within an otherwise mature environment --
not as rescuing a broken organization or building where nothing existed.

## Mid-Size Scale-Up

**Use when:** The target company is growth-stage biotech, OR any company undergoing
significant transformation regardless of current size or maturity. The signal is
organizational state, not headcount.

**Do NOT use when:** The company is stable, scaled, and operating in steady state.

**Framing emphasis:** Archetype governs achievement selection and framing. This
guidance adjusts how the organizational environment and operating context are
described -- not how achievements are characterized.

The target organization is in a growth or transformation state. Frame the operating
context accordingly -- you are entering an environment where capability, process,
and infrastructure are being built or significantly evolved. Emphasize your ability
to operate with ambiguity, build where structure is absent, and design for scale
ahead of organizational growth. Outcomes should reflect your role in positioning
the organization for its next stage of maturity. Where steady-state process
optimization is present in your experience, de-emphasize it in favor of the
building and scaling work that reads as relevant to this context.
