# Story Format: ATOLA

Behavioral interview story format used by AstraZeneca and similar organizations. Emphasizes thinking process and learning over action sequence. Use when the interviewer or company explicitly requests ATOLA, or when the most differentiated part of the story is the reasoning and judgment behind the actions rather than the actions themselves.

---

## Format

Every entry carries a 5-line metadata block directly under the `## [Title]` heading, before any section:

```
Tags: [pipe-separated Capability values]
Archetype: [pipe-separated Archetype values]
Era: [single Era value]
Added: YYYY-MM
Last Used:
```

Controlled values come from the Tag Taxonomy header at the top of `knowledge/Career_Narratives.md`. `Added` is stamped at creation (current month). `Last Used` is left blank by the builder and stamped by output-producing skills at session close.

### Actions
What was done. Describe the key actions taken in the situation. Be specific — this is not a summary of the situation but an account of what you did.

### Thinking
The reasoning behind the actions. What was considered, what was questioned, what was rejected and why. This is the depth section — what sets ATOLA apart from STAR is that thinking is treated as a first-class field, not collapsed into Action. Include: how the problem was diagnosed, what assumptions were tested, and how competing priorities or constraints were weighed.

### Outcomes
What resulted. Use the strongest available evidence: quantified metrics first, then bounded qualitative claims, then before/after state descriptions. Avoid vague category labels.

### Learnings
What you learned from the experience. Be honest — learnings that only confirm what you already knew are not learnings. The strongest entries include something that changed your approach going forward.

### Applications
How the learnings were applied in subsequent work. Connects the story to future behavior — demonstrates that the learning was genuine and durable.

---

## Guidance Notes

- ATOLA is favored by companies that emphasize evidence of learning agility and self-awareness in leadership candidates.
- If an ATOLA entry and a STAR entry are both needed for the same situation, do not duplicate — write ATOLA and note that the STAR version can be derived from Actions and Outcomes.
- The Thinking section should not be a list of tasks. It should read as reasoning — "I questioned whether... because..." or "I rejected X on the basis that..."
- Appropriate length for a live interview answer: 2-3 minutes. Written entries may be longer to capture full depth.
