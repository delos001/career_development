# Story Format: STAR

Standard behavioral interview story format. Widely recognized across industries and interviewers. Use when the situation is relatively self-contained and the emphasis is on what was done and what resulted.

---

## Format

Every entry carries a 5-line metadata block directly under the `## [Title]` heading, before any section:

```
Tags: [pipe-separated Capability values]
Archetype: [pipe-separated Archetype values]
Era: [single Era value]
Added: YYYY-MM
Last Used:
```

Controlled values come from the Tag Taxonomy header at the top of `knowledge/Career_Narratives.md`. `Added` is stamped at creation (current month). `Last Used` is left blank by the builder and stamped by output-producing skills at session close.

### Situation
Set the context. Describe the organizational environment, what existed or was happening, and what created the need for action. Include scale indicators where relevant — scope of the problem, who was affected, what was at stake.

### Task
What you were responsible for. What success looked like and why it mattered.

### Action
What you specifically did. Focus on your individual contribution and key decisions made. This is the depth section — do not reduce it to a task list. Include how resistance or constraints were navigated where relevant.

### Result
What happened as a direct result of your actions. Use the strongest available evidence: quantified metrics first, then bounded qualitative claims ("first," "only," "eliminated"), then before/after state descriptions. Avoid vague category labels such as "improved efficiency" or "enhanced quality."

---

## Guidance Notes

- STAR is the most widely recognized format. When in doubt about what format an interviewer expects, STAR is the safe default.
- The Action section carries the most weight. Interviewers listen for evidence of judgment, not just effort.
- Result must describe what changed — not what was produced. A report delivered is not a result; what the report enabled or changed is.
- Appropriate length for a live interview answer: 2-3 minutes. Written entries may be longer to capture full depth.
