# Decision Format: Personal

Expanded decision documentation format optimized for interview depth. Captures the full reasoning arc behind a professional decision — alternatives considered, criteria applied, pushback received, and honest retrospective. Use when the decision had genuine consequences, alternatives were non-trivial, and the reasoning process itself demonstrates judgment worth conveying in an interview.

---

## Format

Every entry carries a 5-line metadata block directly under the `## [Title]` heading, before any section:

```
Tags: [pipe-separated Capability values]
Archetype: [pipe-separated Archetype values]
Era: [single Era value]
Added: YYYY-MM
Last Used:
```

Controlled values come from the Tag Taxonomy header at the top of `knowledge/Career_Narratives.md`. `Added` is stamped at creation (current month). `Last Used` is left blank by the builder and stamped by output-producing skills at session close.

### The Decision
A single clear statement of what was decided. One or two sentences maximum. The rest of the entry explains it — this field states it.

### The Context
The situation that forced the decision. What was at stake, why the choice was non-trivial, and what would have happened without a decision or with a default one. Include relevant organizational pressures, timing constraints, or dependencies.

### The Options and What Each Cost
Each real alternative that was on the table, stated specifically. For each: what it offered and what it cost or risked. Do not present straw-man alternatives that were never genuinely considered. The strength of a decision entry depends on the honesty and specificity of the alternatives.

### My Criteria
The standards used to evaluate options. What mattered most in this decision and why. If criteria conflicted, state how they were prioritized.

### My Reasoning
Why the chosen option was selected over the alternatives. The logic and evidence, not just the conclusion. If the reasoning relied on an assumption or prediction that could have been wrong, say so.

### Who Pushed Back
Who disagreed with the decision, what their position was, and whether it was legitimate. Honest assessment is required — dismissing all opposition as uninformed or political is not credible. If the pushback identified a real risk, acknowledge it.

### The Outcome
What happened as a result. Whether the decision proved correct, proved wrong, or produced mixed results. If the outcome is not yet known, state that.

### What I'd Own Differently
An honest assessment of what was handled suboptimally, even if the decision itself was correct. A perfect decision entry with no ownable reflection is not credible and does not demonstrate learning. This field is not about failure — it is about growth.

---

## Guidance Notes

- This format is designed for situations where the reasoning process is as important as the outcome. Interviewers asking "tell me about a difficult decision" are assessing judgment, not just results.
- The Options field is the hardest to write honestly. The temptation is to present the chosen option as obviously correct from the start — resist this. If there were real tradeoffs, state them.
- What I'd Own Differently must be genuine. It should describe something that actually could have been done better, not a cosmetic acknowledgment of imperfection.
