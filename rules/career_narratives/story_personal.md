# Story Format: Personal (Expanded)

Expanded story format optimized for depth of capture. Includes fields not present in STAR or ATOLA that are useful for CV generation (Scale, Value Translation) and for interview prep (Tradeoff, Constraints, Baseline). Use when capturing a situation for the first time and wanting to preserve maximum detail for future use. STAR or ATOLA answers can be derived from this format without information loss.

---

## Format

Every entry carries a 5-line metadata block directly under the `## [Title]` heading, before any section:

```
Tags: [pipe-separated Capability values]
Archetype: [pipe-separated Archetype values]
Era: [single Era value]
Added: YYYY-MM
Last Used:
```

Controlled values come from the Tag Taxonomy header at the top of `knowledge/Career_Narratives.md`. `Added` is stamped at creation (current month). `Last Used` is left blank by the builder and stamped by output-producing skills at session close.

### Situation
The organizational context, what was inherited or observed, and what created the need for action.

### Baseline
The measurable or observable starting state before any action was taken. Quantitative where available; descriptive where not. Establishes the before-state against which outcomes are assessed.

### Task
What was required — what success looked like and why it was needed.

### Action
What was done and how. Include key decisions made, alternatives considered, and how resistance or constraints were navigated. Do not reduce to a task list — the judgment and approach are what matter here.

### My Role
The specific roles played: developer, designer, lead, influencer, decision-maker. Be precise. Do not claim credit for work done by others.

### Thinking
The reasoning behind the approach chosen. What was questioned, what was confirmed, what was rejected and why.

### Tradeoff
What was sacrificed or accepted in exchange for the chosen approach. What pivots were made and what was given up to make them.

### Constraints / Mitigation
Limitations encountered — resource, political, technical, timeline. For each constraint, how it was addressed.

### Outcome
What changed — structurally, operationally, or organizationally. Use the strongest available evidence: quantified metrics first, then bounded qualitative claims, then before/after state descriptions. Avoid vague category labels.

### Value Translation
Why the outcome mattered beyond the immediate task — patient impact, organizational capability, regulatory risk, strategic positioning, or competitive advantage.

### Scale
The scope of the work: studies, vendors, functions, geographies, headcount, programs, or budget affected. Scale establishes the significance of the work independent of the outcome.

### Learnings
What would be done differently with hindsight or unlimited resources.

### Application
How the learnings were applied in subsequent work.

---

## Guidance Notes

- This format is designed for written capture, not live delivery. Derive STAR or ATOLA answers from this entry when needed for interview use.
- The Baseline field is particularly valuable for CV generation — it establishes the before-state that makes the outcome legible.
- Value Translation and Scale are the fields most useful for CV bullet construction and should not be left blank if the story is likely to produce a CV entry.
- My Role must be honest about scope and contribution. Inflated role claims are detectable in interviews.
