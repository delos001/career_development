# Archetype 2: Data & Analytics

## 1. Purpose and Scope

Apply this skeleton when the confirmed archetype is Data & Analytics. Load alongside:

- The active domain pack (declared in `personal/knowledge/Experience_Inventory.md` header). Supplies match criteria, summary content, tag priorities, calibration examples, and de-emphasis content for this archetype at the confirmed level.
- The level-specific content rules file.
  - Leadership roles: `rules/cv/content_rules_leadership.md`
  - IC roles: `rules/cv/content_rules_ic.md`

## 2. Archetype Identity

A role fits this archetype when the primary deliverable is data-driven insight, analytical capability, or a governed data or analytics operating model. Characteristic contexts include building analytics functions, establishing data governance, delivering insights that shape decisions, and designing data acquisition or quality frameworks. The archetype applies both when the candidate directs the analytical capability and when the candidate is the hands-on practitioner producing the analysis.

The archetype applies at both Leadership and IC levels. Scope and authority claims adjust per the level rules. Specific match criteria and framing language are supplied by the active domain pack.

## 3. Structural Rules

Required Section Order:

1. Professional Summary
2. Core Competencies
3. Professional Experience
4. Selected Projects (include when entries exist in the Independent & Volunteer Projects inventory section; omit if empty)
5. Education
6. Certifications and Training
7. Technical Proficiencies (positioned last but given more weight than in other archetypes; see Section 5)

Professional Summary structure: 3 to 4 sentences, four required elements. Specific lead-with, do-not-lead-with, register, and required-element content come from the domain pack.

Universal framing pattern: challenge or problem, approach, outcome. Level-general framing emphasis is governed by the active content rules file. Archetype-specific framing language is supplied by the domain pack.

Achievement line count: 2 lines standard. 3 lines acceptable for the highest-complexity achievements only. 4 or more lines never acceptable.

## 5. Archetype-Specific Handling: Technical Credibility

This archetype has a recurring tension: the candidate's technical depth (data science credentials, programming languages, ML frameworks) is a genuine differentiator, but leading with it positions the candidate as a practitioner rather than a strategic leader (at Leadership level) or overstates analytical maturity (at IC level). Apply these pattern rules regardless of domain:

- Core Competencies span three zones: (1) data strategy and governance (4 to 5 items at Leadership; adjusted per level rules at IC), (2) analytics capability and operating model (3 to 4 items), (3) technical credibility (2 to 3 items). The technical zone confirms hands-on literacy without dominating.

- Technical credentials (degrees, certifications) belong in Education or Certifications. They can be referenced once in the summary as a credentialing signal. They should not drive achievement framing.

- Technical Proficiencies carries more weight than in other archetypes. Expand modestly to confirm hands-on literacy without elaborating on proficiency levels or specific applications. The section signals capability; the experience section demonstrates leadership.

- If a role description emphasizes hands-on delivery alongside strategic leadership, Technical Proficiencies can expand by one paragraph. Do not expand beyond that without flagging a potential level mismatch in the fit assessment.

- Specific languages, environments, and platforms to include are supplied by the domain pack under Technical Proficiencies content.
