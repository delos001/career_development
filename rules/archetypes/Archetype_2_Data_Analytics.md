# Archetype 2: Data & Analytics

## Identity

A role fits this archetype when the primary deliverable is data-driven insight, analytical capability, or a governed data or analytics operating model. Characteristic contexts: building analytics functions, establishing data governance, delivering insights that shape decisions, and designing data acquisition or quality frameworks. Applies both when the candidate directs the analytical capability and when the candidate is the hands-on practitioner producing the analysis.

Applies at both Leadership and IC levels. Voice and register come from the active content rules file; vocabulary, tech stack, and calibration examples come from the active domain file.

## Match Criteria

**Leadership:** data strategy, data governance, analytics capability development, data science enablement (infrastructure, processes, or governance for reliable operation at scale), analytics operating model design.

**IC:** hands-on data management or data quality review, individual analytical delivery (models, scripts, reports, statistical analysis), data science application, data quality monitoring and root cause analysis, regulatory or compliance data work at the dataset or deliverable level.

Verify against Archetype 4 if the primary output is a technology platform. Verify against Archetype 3 if data work is incidental to process standardization.

## Summary Framing

- **Lead with:** data strategy, governance, analytics capability leadership (Leadership) or data expertise, analytical depth, technical execution (IC)
- **Do NOT lead with:** programming languages, ML methods, specific tools, or hands-on data work (Leadership); data strategy ownership or governance authority (IC)
- **Register:** Leadership = strategic data leader building decision-relevant, governed analytics capabilities. IC = technically capable practitioner delivering reliable, decision-relevant analytical work.
- **Required summary elements:** years in the active domain, core capability signal per match criteria, evidence of scale or depth (Leadership: cross-functional governance, enterprise standards, executive influence; IC: specific methods, environments, problem types), forward-looking statement tying track record to the role's implied analytical need.

## Achievement Framing Pattern

**Leadership:** data quality risk, governance gap, or analytical maturity problem → governance, strategy, or capability solution → decision-relevant or organizational outcome. Do not lead with technical solution or artifact. Outcome describes organizational impact (decisions improved, risks reduced, scale enabled), not technical performance.

**IC:** data problem or analytical need → technical approach or solution → concrete outcome or impact. Methods, tools, and approaches are appropriate when they explain how the outcome was achievable. Outcome stated in concrete impact terms (what improved, what risk reduced, what decision enabled), not what was built.

## Archetype-Specific Handling: Technical Credibility

Technical depth is a differentiator, but leading with it mispositions the candidate. Apply regardless of domain:

- Core Competencies span three zones: (1) data strategy and governance (4 to 5 items at Leadership; adjusted per level rules at IC), (2) analytics capability and operating model (3 to 4 items), (3) technical credibility (2 to 3 items). The technical zone confirms hands-on literacy without dominating.
- Technical credentials (degrees, certifications) belong in Education or Certifications. They may be referenced once in the summary as a credentialing signal. They do not drive achievement framing.
- Technical Proficiencies carries more weight than in Archetypes 1 and 3. At Leadership, expand modestly to confirm hands-on literacy without elaborating on proficiency levels or applications. At IC, list fully and accurately per source documents.
- If a role emphasizes hands-on delivery alongside strategic leadership, Technical Proficiencies may expand by one paragraph. Do not expand further without flagging potential level mismatch.
- Specific languages, environments, ML frameworks, and tools for Technical Proficiencies come from the active domain file.

## De-Emphasize

**Leadership:** IC-level analytical work in recent roles (reframe at governance/strategy or exclude); operational or trial management achievements unless they informed a data strategy decision; process standardization without data governance framing (route to Archetype 3); summary mentions of specific tools or languages.

**IC:** data strategy and governance ownership language; summary references to analytics capability leadership; organizational scope inflation not supported by source documents.
