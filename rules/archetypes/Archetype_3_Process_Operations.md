# Archetype 3: Process & Operations

## Identity

A role fits this archetype when the primary deliverable is operational efficiency, standardization, quality, or process excellence. Characteristic contexts: SOP design and maintenance, process harmonization across functions or sites, steady-state operational management, inspection or audit readiness, and optimization of established ways of working. Emphasizes durable process improvement rather than organizational transformation or new capability construction.

Applies at both Leadership and IC levels. Voice and register come from the active content rules file; vocabulary and calibration examples come from the active domain file.

## Match Criteria

**Leadership:** process design, standardization, or harmonization in regulated or structured environments; operational efficiency, Lean/Six Sigma, or continuous improvement leadership; framework development, SOP governance, inspection readiness, or controlled documentation; cross-functional process ownership with risk management or quality systems emphasis; vendor or partner oversight models and external interface process management.

**IC:** individual process design, SOP development, or procedure writing; operational execution and quality (following, improving, or troubleshooting processes); Lean/Six Sigma application at project or functional level (process mapping, root cause analysis, waste reduction); inspection readiness support; operational problem-solving at the trial, site, or deliverable level.

Verify against Archetype 4 if the role centers on technology platform ownership. Verify against Archetype 2 if data strategy is the primary subject. Verify against Archetype 1 if the role is primarily organizational transformation at enterprise scope.

## Summary Framing

- **Lead with:** process and governance expertise, cross-functional influence, operational impact (Leadership) or process design expertise, operational problem-solving, execution in regulated environments (IC)
- **Do NOT lead with:** technical capabilities, data science credentials, programming languages, or analytical tools (Leadership); cross-functional ownership or enterprise governance authority (IC)
- **Register:** Leadership = strategic leader building scalable processes and governance structures, not a technical optimizer. IC = skilled practitioner designing, implementing, and improving processes and procedures.
- **Required summary elements:** years in the active domain, core capability signal per match criteria, evidence of scope or organizational impact (Leadership: global, cross-functional, enterprise, or multi-program scope; IC: types of processes, functional areas engaged, operational problems addressed), forward-looking statement tying track record to the role's implied process or governance need.

## Inventory Tag Priorities

Level filter on queries comes from the active content rules file.

**Leadership High priority (at least two must be coverable):**
- Capability: Process Design & Optimization
- Capability: Governance & Risk Management; Context: Mature/Enterprise or Scaling
- Capability: Stakeholder Management & Influence
- Outcome: Risk Reduction OR Outcome: Efficiency Gain (at least one must appear with a metric or organizational result)

**Leadership Medium:**
- Strategic Planning & Roadmapping only when the roadmap is process- or governance-focused
- Change Management & Adoption; Context: Scaling or Greenfield (process adoption at scale, not organizational transformation)
- Outcome: Quality Improvement only if tied to a framework, governance structure, or SOP

**Leadership Avoid:**
- Technology Implementation unless it directly enabled a process or governance capability
- IC-level achievements without process or framework outcome
- Purely analytical achievements without operational or organizational impact

**IC High priority (at least two must be coverable):**
- Capability: Process Design & Optimization; Outcome: Efficiency Gain OR Risk Reduction
- Capability: Process Design & Optimization; Outcome: Quality Improvement
- Capability: Quality & Compliance (inspection readiness, SOP governance, controlled documentation)

**IC Medium:**
- Process Design & Optimization AND Change Management & Adoption (process improvements requiring rollout or training)
- Vendor Management & Oversight (process contributions involving external partner coordination)
- Trial or operational execution capability tags in context (operational execution depth in the active domain)

**IC Avoid:**
- Technology Implementation without a process outcome (route to Archetype 4 unless primary output was a process enabled by the technology)
- Organizational design or enterprise governance language
- Summary references to strategic process leadership not supported by source documents

## Achievement Framing Pattern

**Leadership:** business problem or operational risk → process, framework, or governance solution → measurable or organizational outcome. Problem first. Do not lead with solution or technical execution. Outcome is organizational or measurable, not a description of an artifact.

**IC:** operational problem or process gap → specific process or framework designed or improved → measurable or operational outcome. Name the specific process, procedure, or framework. Technical execution detail is appropriate at IC level. Outcome stated in concrete terms (what improved, what risk reduced, what was standardized).

Calibration examples for this archetype are provided in the active domain file.

## De-Emphasize

- Technical skills section kept short; list only, no proficiency levels or applications (Leadership)
- Programming languages and ML or modeling work unless directly producing a process or governance capability
- Data science framing in the summary (route to Archetype 2)
- Tool-specific achievements (route to Archetype 4)
- Cross-functional ownership and enterprise governance language at IC level
- Organizational design signals at IC unless the specific contribution was the design work
