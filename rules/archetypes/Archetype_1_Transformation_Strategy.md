# Archetype 1: Transformation & Strategy

## Identity

A role fits this archetype when the primary deliverable is an organizational capability or structural change, not a product, process, or technology. Characteristic contexts: greenfield capability building, operating model redesign, enterprise-scope change programs, and turnaround scenarios where an existing model must be rebuilt rather than optimized.

Applies at both Leadership and IC levels. Level governs scope of claim and voice (owning the transformation vs. delivering within it) per the active content rules file. Vocabulary and calibration examples come from the active domain file.

## Match Criteria

**Leadership:** enterprise-level organizational change, greenfield capability building, operating model redesign, large-scale change programs requiring stakeholder navigation and cultural adoption, transformation with accountability for adoption outcomes.

**IC:** execution within a transformation program (workstreams, adoption activities, change enablement), readiness or training materials, cross-functional coordination supporting capability-building, analytical or design contributions informing transformation decisions, project management within a transformation context.

Verify against Archetype 3 if the role centers on standardization or steady-state efficiency. Verify against Archetype 4 if the role centers on platform ownership. If the role is primarily maintaining or optimizing an existing operating model, this archetype is wrong.

## Summary Framing

- **Lead with:** transformation, capability building, enterprise change leadership (Leadership) or transformation program execution and change enablement (IC)
- **Do NOT lead with:** process optimization, technical capabilities, data or analytics credentials, steady-state operational management
- **Register:** Leadership = builder of new organizational capabilities, stakeholder navigator, cultural adoption driver. IC = skilled practitioner delivering within transformation programs.
- **Required summary elements:** years in the active domain, core capability signal per match criteria, evidence of complexity (resistance, cultural shift, multi-function or multi-region scope), forward-looking statement tying track record to the role's implied transformation need.

## Inventory Tag Priorities

Level filter on queries comes from the active content rules file.

**High priority (at least two must be coverable; flag otherwise):**
- Capability: Change Management & Adoption; Context: Greenfield OR Scaling
- Capability: Organizational Design; Outcome: Capability Building; Context: Greenfield OR Turnaround
- Capability: Stakeholder Management & Influence; Context: Scaling OR Turnaround
- Outcome: Capability Building; Context: Greenfield (at least one achievement must show building from scratch)

**Medium priority:**
- Strategic Planning & Roadmapping only when the roadmap subject is an organizational capability, not a platform or process framework
- Process Design & Optimization (Context: Greenfield) only for new capability construction
- Team Leadership & Development only for building a new team or capability

**Use if space permits:**
- Governance & Risk Management only where the structure itself was a new capability
- Financial Management & Budgeting only for resource orchestration at transformation scale

**Avoid:**
- Context: Mature/Enterprise without Greenfield or Turnaround
- Technology Implementation without change or adoption framing
- IC-level achievements without organizational scope (Leadership only)
- Outcome: Efficiency Gain or Risk Reduction as primary outcomes

## Achievement Framing Pattern

Organizational challenge → change strategy and stakeholder navigation → adoption or capability outcome at scale. Challenge comes first. Outcome describes adoption, capability, or structural change, not process efficiency or technical performance.

Transformation achievement elements (compress into 2 lines; do not expand):
- **Challenge:** organizational problem, growth pressure, or capability gap
- **Strategy:** change approach, coalition, phased design
- **Execution:** how resistance was navigated or what enabled adoption
- **Outcome:** sustained capability or scale metric confirming adoption

Calibration examples for this archetype are provided in the active domain file.

## De-Emphasize

- Process optimization language in summary or leading achievements (route to Archetype 3)
- Analytical or data science work unless it directly informed a transformation decision (route to Archetype 2)
- Steady-state management without greenfield, scaling, or turnaround context
- Incremental improvements framed as transformation
