**ARCHETYPE 4 IC: PLATFORM & TECHNOLOGY**

*CV Generation Instruction Set --- Jason Delosh*

**1. Purpose and Scope**

Apply this document when the archetype is Platform & Technology and the role level confirmed in Phase 2a is Individual Contributor. Use in combination with `rules/cv/content_rules_ic.md`, which governs voice, register, and formatting. Do not apply leadership framing from `Archetype_4_Platform_Technology.md` for IC roles.

**2. Archetype Match Criteria**

Confirm this is the correct archetype before proceeding. The role must center on one or more of the following:

-   Clinical systems configuration, setup, or support --- EDC, CTMS, IXRS/IRT, RBM tools, or document management systems

-   Technology implementation participation --- contributing to system builds, integrations, or deployments in a regulated clinical environment

-   System testing, validation, or UAT in a clinical technology context

-   Clinical domain expertise applied to technology work --- translating operational requirements into system configurations, advising developers, or evaluating platform fit

-   Data flow and interoperability at the system level --- understanding how clinical data moves across platforms and contributing to the integrity of those flows

Critical distinction: this archetype applies when the role's primary work is hands-on with clinical systems --- configuring, implementing, testing, or supporting them. It is not the correct archetype for roles primarily about technology strategy or platform governance ownership (those are above IC level). If the role centers on individual process or SOP work without a technology component, verify against the Process & Operations IC archetype.

**3. Required Section Order**

Build the CV in exactly this section order:

-   1\. Professional Summary

-   2\. Core Competencies

-   3\. Professional Experience

-   4\. Selected Projects (include when entries exist in the Independent & Volunteer Projects inventory section; omit if section is empty)

-   5\. Education

-   6\. Certifications and Training

-   7\. Technical Proficiencies (last section --- this section carries the most weight of any archetype at IC level; expand to fully represent clinical systems, platforms, languages, and environments per `rules/cv/content_rules_ic.md`)

Core Competencies should list 8 to 10 items spanning two zones: (1) clinical systems and technology implementation --- 5 to 6 items, (2) domain knowledge and analytical credibility --- 3 to 4 items. The clinical systems zone is the priority. Do not list technology strategy ownership, enterprise platform governance, or vendor ecosystem management --- these are leadership-level signals.

**4. Inventory Tag Priorities**

Query the Experience Inventory using these tag combinations in priority order. Do not filter by Role Level --- entries from any role level are eligible. The achievement framing rules in Section 3 govern how entries are presented at IC level.

Note: as with the leadership Platform & Technology archetype, the Experience Inventory does not have a dedicated Platform & Technology capability tag. Technology-related achievements are tagged under Capability: Technology Implementation, often with companion tags. Apply compound queries below.

These tag combinations must map to at least one achievement in the finished CV. If fewer than two are coverable from the inventory, flag the gap explicitly before generating.

-   Capability: Technology Implementation --- Outcome: Capability Building OR Scalability/Growth Enablement --- use for system implementations or configurations that delivered a new or materially improved operational capability

-   Capability: Technology Implementation --- Outcome: Risk Reduction OR Quality Improvement --- use for system contributions that reduced data risk, improved data integrity, or supported compliance

-   Capability: Technology Implementation AND Capability: Data & Analytics --- use for platform or system work where Jason's domain knowledge of clinical data requirements was the differentiating contribution

**Avoid for This Archetype**

-   Organizational governance and strategy language --- do not imply Jason owned the technology direction, led vendor selection, or directed enterprise platform decisions

-   Capability: Process Design & Optimization without a Technology Implementation tag --- pure process achievements belong in the Process & Operations IC archetype

**5. Summary Framing Rules**

**Lead with:** Clinical systems expertise, technology implementation experience, and domain knowledge in regulated clinical environments

**Do NOT lead with:** Technology strategy ownership, enterprise platform governance, or vendor ecosystem leadership

**Register:** Technically capable practitioner with deep clinical systems knowledge who implements, configures, supports, and improves technology platforms in regulated clinical environments

**Length:** 3 to 4 sentences only

Required elements --- all four must be present:

-   Years of experience and domain (clinical technology environments, regulated pharma/biotech, sponsor or CRO context)

-   Core capability signal --- one of: clinical systems implementation, EDC or CTMS expertise, system integration, data flow and interoperability, or technology configuration and support

-   Evidence of technical or domain depth: specific systems, platforms, or implementation contexts handled

-   One forward-looking statement connecting Jason's clinical systems and technology experience to the specific platform need stated or implied in the job description

**3. Achievement Framing Rules**

Every achievement must follow this structure:

Technology problem, system need, or platform limitation -\> Specific technical contribution or implementation work -\> Outcome or impact on system performance, data quality, or operational capability

Technical detail is appropriate and expected at IC level. Specific systems, configurations, and integrations should be named where they explain the outcome. The outcome must still be stated in impact terms --- what was enabled, what risk was reduced, what capability was delivered --- not just what was configured.

Every achievement must be expressed within 2 lines. 3 lines is acceptable only for the highest-complexity achievements where technical or outcome detail cannot be compressed further. 4 or more lines is never acceptable.

**Calibration Examples**

**WEAK --- describes task without context or outcome:**

*\"Configured RAVE EDC system for new clinical studies, coordinating with data management and clinical operations to ensure system setup met protocol requirements.\"*

**STRONG --- technology problem first, specific contribution, concrete outcome:**

*\"Identified that legacy macro-driven data processes could not scale with portfolio growth and provided clinical domain input to a cross-functional platform evaluation. Contributed to the enterprise selection of Databricks to replace legacy infrastructure by grounding the evaluation in operational data requirements.\"*

**REFRAME --- same advisory work, contribution and impact clearly stated:**

*\"Translated field-level risk-based monitoring workflows into platform feature requirements for an RBM technology vendor, ensuring the resulting tool was grounded in field-level monitoring practice rather than abstract design assumptions.\"*

**7. Content to De-emphasize**

-   Technology strategy ownership and enterprise platform governance language --- do not imply Jason directed the technology strategy, led enterprise system selection, or governed the vendor ecosystem. Reframe to the specific system contribution and its outcome.

-   Summary references to platform leadership or technology strategic direction --- the summary positions Jason as a technically capable clinical systems practitioner, not a technology strategist.

-   Organizational scope inflation --- do not imply enterprise-level technology accountability or executive technology advisory authority not present in source documents.

-   Technical Proficiencies section: expand fully. For this archetype at IC level, the Technical Proficiencies section is the most important section after Professional Experience. Clinical systems, platforms, languages, environments, and tools must be listed completely and accurately. Do not compress or minimize.
