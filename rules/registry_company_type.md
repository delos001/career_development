# Company Type Registry

Used by `interview_prep` to determine adaptive research branches. Once the company type is identified from available context, execute all branches listed under that type plus the Universal branches that apply to all types.

---

## Universal — All Company Types

Execute these branches for every company regardless of type:

- Mission, values, and stated strategic priorities
- Leadership team: CEO and relevant functional leaders (names, tenure, background)
- Financial health and recent performance (public filings, analyst coverage, or news if private)
- Recent news: acquisitions, partnerships, restructuring, layoffs, expansions
- Market position and competitive landscape
- Culture signals: Glassdoor, LinkedIn patterns, press coverage of workplace

---

## CRO / Clinical Research Organization

Execute in addition to Universal:

- Full-service vs FSP model, or hybrid — and which this role sits within
- Therapy area focus and major client relationships (where publicly known)
- Regulatory inspection posture: any public 483s, warning letters, or consent decrees
- Technology and platform investments (EDC, eClinical, AI/ML initiatives)
- Pipeline exposure if the CRO has equity or milestone arrangements

---

## Pharma / Large Biopharma

Execute in addition to Universal:

- Therapy area portfolio and pipeline stage distribution
- Key products: commercial and late-stage assets
- Recent R&D strategy shifts or therapeutic area exits
- Regulatory posture: any recent approval decisions, CRL responses, or post-market issues
- M&A activity and integration status if applicable
- Operational model: insourced vs outsourced clinical operations

---

## Biotech — Clinical Stage

Execute in addition to Universal:

- Lead asset: indication, trial phase, recent data readouts or upcoming milestones
- Pipeline breadth beyond lead asset
- Funding stage: Series, IPO status, or public float
- Cash runway and burn rate (if determinable from public sources)
- Key investors and board composition signals
- Regulatory interactions: Breakthrough Designation, Fast Track, any CRL history

---

## Biotech — Pre-Clinical / Early Stage

Execute in addition to Universal:

- Platform technology and scientific thesis
- Funding history and lead investors
- Key scientific advisors or academic partnerships
- Proximity to IND and clinical entry
- Team composition signals (build-out stage)

---

## Technology / SaaS / Health Tech Platform

Execute in addition to Universal:

- Core product and platform capabilities
- Revenue model: subscription, usage-based, enterprise license
- Customer base and key verticals served
- Competitive landscape and primary competitors
- Product roadmap signals from press, job postings, or founder interviews
- Regulatory exposure if operating in regulated markets (FDA, HIPAA, EMA)

---

## Consulting / Professional Services

Execute in addition to Universal:

- Practice areas and service lines relevant to this role
- Client base and industry concentration
- Firm structure: partnership track, utilization expectations, travel norms
- Recent notable engagements or public case studies
- Differentiation vs peers in the same space

---

## Academic Medical Center / Health System

Execute in addition to Universal:

- System size and regional footprint
- Research vs clinical mission balance
- Funding sources: NIH, industry-sponsored, philanthropy
- Technology infrastructure and known transformation initiatives
- Regulatory and compliance environment

---

## Government / Regulatory Body

Execute in addition to Universal:

- Agency mission and relevant division or center
- Current regulatory priorities and policy direction
- Recent high-profile decisions or enforcement actions in relevant area
- Career structure and advancement norms
- Known funding constraints or workforce dynamics

---

## Adding New Types

When a company does not fit any existing type, create a new entry following the format above. Add the type name, a one-line description of when it applies, and the specific research branches that are relevant beyond Universal. Do not force a poor-fit match to an existing type.
