**ARCHETYPE 1 IC: TRANSFORMATION & STRATEGY**

*CV Generation Instruction Set --- Jason Delosh*

**1. Purpose and Scope**

Apply this document when the archetype is Transformation & Strategy and the role level confirmed in Phase 2a is Individual Contributor. Use in combination with `context/cv_content_rules_ic.md`, which governs voice, register, and formatting. Do not apply leadership framing from `Archetype_1_Transformation_Strategy.md` for IC roles.

**2. Archetype Match Criteria**

Confirm this is the correct archetype before proceeding. The role must center on one or more of the following:

-   Execution within a transformation program --- delivering workstreams, coordinating adoption activities, or enabling change at the project or functional level

-   Change enablement support --- developing training materials, communication plans, stakeholder engagement assets, or readiness assessments within a larger change initiative

-   Cross-functional coordination in support of an operating model or capability-building effort --- not ownership of the program

-   Analytical or design contributions that inform transformation decisions --- process mapping, gap analysis, current-state assessment, or adoption tracking

-   Program or project management within a transformation context --- not strategic direction-setting

If the role centers primarily on individual process design or SOP development without a transformation or change program context, verify against the Process & Operations IC archetype. If it centers on individual technology implementation or system configuration, verify against the Platform & Technology IC archetype. This archetype applies when the role's primary context is a change or transformation initiative --- even if Jason's contribution is an individual one.

**3. Required Section Order**

Build the CV in exactly this section order:

-   1\. Professional Summary

-   2\. Core Competencies

-   3\. Professional Experience

-   4\. Education

-   5\. Certifications and Training

-   6\. Technical Proficiencies (last section --- given more weight than in leadership CVs per `context/cv_content_rules_ic.md`)

Core Competencies should list 8 to 10 items and prioritize change enablement, cross-functional coordination, and execution capabilities. Technical and analytical capabilities are appropriate for this archetype at IC level where they directly supported transformation delivery. Do not list organizational design, strategic direction, or enterprise capability-building as competencies --- these are leadership-level signals.

**4. Inventory Tag Priorities**

Query the Experience Inventory using these tag combinations in priority order. Do not filter by Role Level --- entries from any role level are eligible. The achievement framing rules in Section 3 govern how entries are presented at IC level.

**High Priority --- Must Be Represented**

These tag combinations must map to at least one achievement in the finished CV. If fewer than two are coverable from the inventory, flag the gap explicitly before generating.

-   Capability: Change Management & Adoption --- Outcome: Capability Building OR Scalability/Growth Enablement

-   Capability: Stakeholder Management & Influence --- Context: Greenfield OR Scaling

-   Capability: Process Design & Optimization --- Context: Greenfield OR Scaling --- use only where the process contribution was part of a transformation or change initiative

**Medium Priority --- Include Where Space Permits**

-   Capability: Organizational Design --- only if the contribution was a design input (mapping, analysis, recommendation) rather than ownership of the design decision

-   Capability: Strategic Planning & Roadmapping --- only if Jason contributed analytical or coordination work that informed the roadmap, not if he owned it

-   Capability: Team Leadership & Development --- only if it shows enabling or onboarding others as part of a change program, not managing a team

**Use If Space Permits**

-   Capability: Data & Analytics --- only if the analytical work directly supported a transformation decision or adoption tracking effort

-   Capability: Technology Implementation --- only if the technology was being adopted as part of a transformation initiative and Jason's contribution was change enablement, not technical implementation

**Avoid for This Archetype**

-   Capability: Governance & Risk Management without a change or adoption context --- governance work belongs in Process & Operations unless it was itself a transformation deliverable

-   Steady-state operational or process maintenance work --- if the context is Mature/Enterprise without a Greenfield, Scaling, or Turnaround signal, verify relevance before including

-   Outcome: Efficiency Gain as a primary outcome --- route to Process & Operations unless the efficiency gain was a direct consequence of a transformation program

**5. Summary Framing Rules**

**Lead with:** Transformation program experience, change enablement, and cross-functional execution in regulated clinical environments

**Do NOT lead with:** Organizational design authority, enterprise-level strategic direction, or capability-building ownership

**Register:** Skilled practitioner who delivers within transformation programs --- executing workstreams, enabling adoption, and contributing analysis that informs transformation decisions

**Length:** 3 to 4 sentences only

Required elements --- all four must be present:

-   Years of experience and domain (regulated clinical environments, pharma/biotech)

-   Core capability signal --- one of: change enablement, transformation program delivery, stakeholder engagement, or cross-functional coordination

-   Evidence of contribution complexity: scope of work within a larger program, types of stakeholders engaged, or analytical or design contributions made

-   One forward-looking statement connecting Jason's transformation execution experience to the specific program need stated or implied in the job description

**3. Achievement Framing Rules**

Every achievement must follow this structure:

Program need or change challenge -\> Specific contribution or deliverable -\> Outcome or impact on program success

The specific contribution comes from Jason's work, not the program outcome as a whole. Do not claim ownership of enterprise-level outcomes. Do state the impact of the contribution on the program, workstream, or team. Technical execution is appropriate and expected at this level.

Every achievement must be expressed within 2 lines. 3 lines is acceptable only for the highest-complexity achievements where scope or outcome detail cannot be compressed further. 4 or more lines is never acceptable.

**Calibration Examples**

**WEAK --- describes activity without impact:**

*\"Supported change management activities for a clinical data transformation initiative, including preparing training materials and attending stakeholder meetings.\"*

**STRONG --- specific contribution, clear program impact:**

*\"Designed and delivered training infrastructure for a cross-functional data acquisition capability rollout, enabling consistent onboarding across eight study teams. Adoption reached full compliance within the first submission cycle.\"*

**REFRAME --- same work, contribution framed with program impact:**

*\"Developed the stakeholder engagement plan and communication materials that supported a governed data acquisition transition across five functional areas, contributing to a change management approach that produced no disruption to active submission timelines.\"*

**7. Content to De-emphasize**

-   Organizational ownership language --- do not write achievements that imply Jason owned the transformation, designed the change strategy, or directed organizational adoption. These are leadership-level signals that misrepresent IC contribution and create interview exposure.

-   Summary references to enterprise capability-building or operating model redesign --- the summary establishes contribution-level positioning. Program-level outcomes belong only when Jason's individual work demonstrably contributed to them.

-   Strategic direction language --- avoid "defined strategy," "established direction," or "led enterprise adoption" unless explicitly accurate. Replace with "contributed to," "supported," "delivered within," or "enabled."

-   People management signals --- do not list team leadership, direct report management, or organizational development as competencies unless the role explicitly calls for it and source documents support it.

-   Technical tools section: include and give it appropriate weight per `context/cv_content_rules_ic.md`. Do not minimize technical skills for this archetype --- they are a legitimate signal of contribution depth at IC level.
