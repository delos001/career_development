**ARCHETYPE 4: PLATFORM & TECHNOLOGY**

*CV Generation Instruction Set --- Jason Delosh*

**1. Purpose and Scope**

This document is an instruction set for Phase 4a CV content generation. Apply all rules in this document when the archetype identified in Phase 2a is Platform & Technology. Do not apply these rules for any other archetype. If a dual-archetype was identified in Phase 2a, apply primary framing from this document and secondary framing selectively within individual achievements only where both dimensions are genuinely present in the underlying experience.

**2. Archetype Match Criteria**

Confirm this is the correct archetype before proceeding. The role must center on one or more of the following:

-   Technology strategy for clinical or data platforms --- defining direction, evaluating options, and aligning the organization to a technology path

-   Platform implementation leadership --- owning or directing the deployment of enterprise clinical systems, data platforms, or technology ecosystems

-   System integration and interoperability --- governing how clinical, data, and operational systems connect, exchange data, and maintain integrity across a technology ecosystem

-   Vendor and tool ecosystem management --- strategic oversight of external technology partners, platform selections, and build-versus-buy decisions at enterprise scale

-   Technology operating model design --- defining how technology capabilities are governed, supported, and evolved within a regulated clinical organization

Critical distinction: this archetype is for roles where technology is the primary subject --- the platform, the system, the vendor ecosystem, or the technology strategy is what the role owns. It is not the correct archetype for roles where technology is a supporting tool for data governance (Data & Analytics archetype) or process improvement (Process & Operations archetype). If the role description is primarily about driving organizational adoption of a technology change, verify against the Transformation & Strategy archetype before applying these instructions.

**3. Inventory Coverage Notice --- Read Before Proceeding**

> IMPORTANT: The Experience Inventory does not contain a dedicated Platform & Technology capability tag. Technology-related achievements are tagged under Capability: Technology Implementation, often in combination with other capability tags such as Data & Analytics, Strategic Planning & Roadmapping, and Governance & Risk Management. This archetype therefore requires compound tag queries rather than single-tag retrieval. Follow the tag query guidance in Section 6 precisely. If compound queries return fewer than three strong technology-strategy achievements at Director level or above, flag this explicitly in the fit assessment before generating the CV. A thin technology leadership inventory is a real constraint --- do not paper over it with framing.

**4. Summary Framing Rules**

Write the professional summary using all of the following rules without exception:

**Lead with:** Technology strategy, platform leadership, and system governance in regulated clinical environments --- not technical execution, hands-on implementation work, or analytical capabilities

**Do NOT lead with:** Programming languages, data science methods, or operational process work. The summary must read as a technology strategist, not a technical practitioner or process designer.

**Register:** Strategic leader who defines technology direction, governs platform ecosystems, and drives organizational alignment to technology decisions in regulated clinical development --- not an IT implementer or clinical systems administrator

**Length:** 3 to 4 sentences only

Required elements --- all four must be present:

-   Years of experience and domain (clinical technology environments, regulated pharma/biotech, sponsor or CRO context)

-   Core capability signal --- one of: technology strategy, platform governance, clinical systems leadership, vendor ecosystem management, or build-versus-buy decision authority

-   Evidence of strategic or organizational-level technology influence: enterprise platform selections, cross-functional technology governance, or executive-level technology advisory

-   One forward-looking statement connecting Jason\'s technology strategy experience to the specific platform challenge, modernization need, or ecosystem complexity described or implied in the job description

**5. Required Section Order**

Build the CV in exactly this section order:

-   1\. Professional Summary

-   2\. Core Competencies

-   3\. Professional Experience

-   4\. Education

-   5\. Certifications and Training

-   6\. Technical Proficiencies (last section --- expanded relative to Process & Operations and Transformation archetypes, but still positioned last)

Core Competencies should list 8 to 12 items spanning three zones: (1) technology strategy and platform governance --- 4 to 5 items, (2) clinical systems and domain knowledge --- 3 to 4 items, (3) analytical and process credibility --- 2 to 3 items. The third zone signals cross-functional depth without displacing the technology strategy lead. Do not let the third zone expand beyond 3 items.

**6. Inventory Tag Priorities and Compound Query Guidance**

Because the inventory does not have a dedicated Platform & Technology tag, all queries must use compound tag combinations. Single-tag queries on Technology Implementation alone will return too many non-strategic entries. Apply compound queries in the priority order below.

**High Priority --- Must Be Represented**

Run each compound query and select the strongest matching achievement at Director level or above. If fewer than two High Priority achievements are retrievable, flag the gap before generating.

-   Capability: Technology Implementation AND Capability: Strategic Planning & Roadmapping --- Role Level: Senior Manager, Director, or Senior Director --- these entries represent the intersection of technology work and strategic direction-setting, which is the core of this archetype

-   Capability: Technology Implementation AND Capability: Governance & Risk Management --- Outcome: Capability Building OR Scalability/Growth Enablement --- these entries represent governed technology decisions with organizational impact

-   Capability: Technology Implementation AND Capability: Data & Analytics --- Context: Scaling OR Mature/Enterprise --- Role Level: Director or Senior Director --- use for platform decisions that were fundamentally driven by data strategy requirements

**Medium Priority --- Include Where Space Permits**

-   Capability: Technology Implementation AND Capability: Stakeholder Management & Influence --- Role Level: Director or Senior Director --- use to demonstrate cross-functional technology alignment and executive-level technology advisory

-   Capability: Strategic Planning & Roadmapping AND Capability: Technology Implementation --- Context: Greenfield --- use to show technology strategy in a build-from-scratch context, which is the strongest signal for platform leadership roles

-   Capability: Technology Implementation AND Capability: Change Management & Adoption --- use only when the achievement demonstrates governing technology adoption at organizational scale, not individual user training

**Use If Space Permits**

-   Capability: Process Design & Optimization AND Capability: Technology Implementation --- use only when the process was directly enabled by a platform or system decision. Frame around the technology governance outcome, not the process outcome.

-   Capability: Organizational Design AND Capability: Technology Implementation --- use when the achievement shows how a technology decision shaped or required an organizational capability or role structure

**Avoid for This Archetype**

-   Capability: Technology Implementation entries tagged at IC or Manager level without a strategic or governance framing --- hands-on system configuration, EDC setup, or tool deployment without organizational technology decision authority is not relevant at this level

-   Capability: Process Design & Optimization without a Technology Implementation tag --- pure process achievements belong in the Process & Operations archetype

-   Capability: Data & Analytics without a Technology Implementation tag --- pure analytics governance achievements belong in the Data & Analytics archetype

-   Clinical trial execution achievements (trial management, site oversight, CRO management) --- these belong in earlier career context only and should not appear in the main experience section for this archetype

**7. Achievement Selection and Framing Rules**

**Selection Criteria**

Select an achievement only if it satisfies all three criteria:

-   Describes a technology strategy decision, platform governance structure, system selection, or technology operating model --- not a technical implementation task or analytical output

-   States or clearly implies the technology risk, platform limitation, or ecosystem complexity that drove the decision or governance need

-   Includes an organizational scope signal: enterprise platform, cross-functional system governance, multi-program technology impact, or executive-level technology decision influence

**Required Framing Pattern**

Every achievement must follow this structure:

Technology risk, platform limitation, or ecosystem complexity -\> Strategy decision or governance solution -\> Organizational or capability outcome

The technology problem or strategic need must come first. Do not lead with the technology solution, the vendor selected, or the system deployed. The outcome must be stated in organizational terms --- what risk was reduced, what capability was enabled, what scale was achieved --- not in technical performance terms. Every achievement must be expressed within 2 lines. 3 lines is acceptable only for the highest-complexity achievements where scope or outcome detail cannot be compressed further. 4 or more lines is never acceptable.

**Domain Knowledge as a Differentiator**

For Platform & Technology roles in clinical pharma/biotech, deep domain knowledge of the clinical systems landscape is a genuine differentiator that many pure technology leaders lack. Apply these rules to surface it appropriately:

-   The Clinical Systems section of the CV carries more weight for this archetype than any other. Ensure it is complete and current: EDC platforms, CTMS, IXRS/IRT, document management, RBM tools, third-party data systems, and modern data platforms (Databricks, AWS).

-   Achievements that demonstrate understanding of how clinical data flows across a technology ecosystem --- from EDC through data management through submission --- signal domain depth that is valuable for clinical platform roles. Surface this where present in the inventory.

-   The eClinical Solutions RBM software consultant role is relevant for this archetype. It demonstrates technology advisory at the product level --- bringing clinical domain knowledge into technology design. Include it in the main experience section, not just Earlier Professional Roles.

**Calibration Examples**

**WEAK --- describes technical implementation without strategic or governance framing:**

*\"Configured RAVE EDC system for new clinical studies, coordinating with data management and clinical operations to ensure system setup met protocol requirements and data collection standards.\"*

**STRONG --- technology risk first, strategic decision, organizational outcome:**

*\"Identified that legacy macro-driven data processes could not scale with portfolio growth and carried increasing regulatory risk as complexity grew. Led cross-functional platform evaluation balancing change management impact against long-term scalability, driving the enterprise selection of Databricks to replace legacy infrastructure.\"*

**REFRAME --- same advisory work repositioned as technology strategy leadership:**

*\"Brought clinical operations domain expertise into product design cycles for an RBM technology platform, translating field-level workflow requirements into platform features and advising developers on operational implications of design decisions. Ensured the resulting tool was grounded in field-level monitoring practice rather than abstract design assumptions.\"*

**8. Technical Proficiencies Section --- Archetype-Specific Handling**

The Technical Proficiencies section for this archetype is expanded relative to Process & Operations and Transformation, but handled differently from Data & Analytics. Apply these specific rules:

-   Clinical Systems subsection is the priority for this archetype. Ensure all systems are listed and current: EDC, CTMS, IXRS/IRT, RBM tools, document management, third-party data systems, and modern data platforms.

-   Programming languages: include but keep brief --- R, Python, SQL, SAS. These confirm technical literacy for evaluating platform capabilities and governing technical vendors, not practitioner depth.

-   Environments and platforms: include cloud and data platform entries specifically --- Databricks, AWS, Git. Omit academic or legacy environments that do not signal current platform relevance.

-   ML and analytics frameworks: include at the list level only --- Scikit-learn, TensorFlow, Keras. These confirm Jason can assess AI/ML platform requirements, not that he builds models.

-   Visualization tools: include --- Spotfire, Power BI, Tableau --- as signals of analytics platform familiarity.

-   Still exclude: proficiency level descriptors, first-appearance dates, and application narratives. List format only.

-   Still keep the section last in the document.

**9. Content to De-emphasize**

-   Hands-on clinical operations and trial management work --- site oversight, CRO management, and trial execution belong in earlier career context only for this archetype, clearly separated from the main experience section

-   Pure data science or analytical achievements without a technology strategy or platform governance framing --- route these to the Data & Analytics archetype

-   Process optimization without a technology dimension --- route these to the Process & Operations archetype

-   Summary references to data science credentials or analytical methods --- the summary reads as a technology strategist, not a data scientist

-   Tool configuration and system administration work at the hands-on level --- EDC configuration, report building, and script development are earlier career signals and must not appear in the main experience blocks for BioMarin or AbbVie without strategic or governance reframing
