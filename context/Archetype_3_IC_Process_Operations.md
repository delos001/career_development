**ARCHETYPE 3 IC: PROCESS & OPERATIONS**

*CV Generation Instruction Set --- Jason Delosh*

**1. Purpose and Scope**

Apply this document when the archetype is Process & Operations and the role level confirmed in Phase 2a is Individual Contributor. Use in combination with `context/cv_content_rules_ic.md`, which governs voice, register, and formatting. Do not apply leadership framing from `Archetype_3_Process_Operations.md` for IC roles.

**2. Archetype Match Criteria**

Confirm this is the correct archetype before proceeding. The role must center on one or more of the following:

-   Individual process design, SOP development, or procedure writing in a regulated clinical environment

-   Operational execution and quality --- following, improving, or troubleshooting clinical or data management processes

-   Lean/Six Sigma application at the project or functional level --- process mapping, root cause analysis, waste reduction

-   Inspection readiness support --- document preparation, controlled documentation, audit trail maintenance

-   Clinical operations execution --- site coordination, CRO interface management, trial-level operational problem solving

If the role centers primarily on hands-on data analysis or data quality work without a process design or operational component, verify against the Data & Analytics IC archetype. If the role centers on change program delivery, verify against the Transformation & Strategy IC archetype. This archetype applies when the role's primary output is a process artifact, operational procedure, or execution quality improvement --- not a data deliverable or a change program workstream.

**3. Required Section Order**

Build the CV in exactly this section order:

-   1\. Professional Summary

-   2\. Core Competencies

-   3\. Professional Experience

-   4\. Education

-   5\. Certifications and Training

-   6\. Technical Proficiencies (last section --- given more weight than in leadership CVs per `context/cv_content_rules_ic.md`)

Core Competencies should list 8 to 10 items and prioritize process design, operational execution, quality systems, and regulated environment expertise. Lean/Six Sigma credentials and methodological depth are appropriate and prominent here. Do not list cross-functional governance ownership, strategic process leadership, or enterprise operating model design --- these are leadership-level signals.

**4. Inventory Tag Priorities**

Query the Experience Inventory using these tag combinations in priority order. Do not filter by Role Level --- entries from any role level are eligible. The achievement framing rules in Section 3 govern how entries are presented at IC level.

**High Priority --- Must Be Represented**

These tag combinations must map to at least one achievement in the finished CV. If fewer than two are coverable from the inventory, flag the gap explicitly before generating.

-   Capability: Process Design & Optimization --- Outcome: Efficiency Gain OR Risk Reduction

-   Capability: Process Design & Optimization --- Outcome: Quality Improvement

-   Capability: Quality & Compliance --- Context: any --- use for inspection readiness, SOP governance, or controlled documentation contributions

**Medium Priority --- Include Where Space Permits**

-   Capability: Process Design & Optimization AND Capability: Change Management & Adoption --- use when process improvements required active rollout or training for adoption

-   Capability: Clinical Trial Execution --- use to show operational execution depth and domain knowledge in the clinical trial context

-   Capability: Vendor Management & Oversight --- use when the process contribution involved external partner coordination or interface process design

**Use If Space Permits**

-   Capability: Data & Analytics AND Capability: Process Design & Optimization --- use only when the analytical work directly produced a process or operational outcome. Frame around the process result, not the analysis.

-   Capability: Risk-Based Monitoring & Quality --- use for monitoring process contributions, risk-based oversight procedures, or quality signal identification work

**Avoid for This Archetype**

-   Capability: Technology Implementation without a process outcome --- tool implementation belongs in Platform & Technology unless the primary output was a process enabled by the technology

-   Organizational design or enterprise governance language --- reframe achievements to the specific process contribution and its operational impact

-   Summary references to strategic process leadership or cross-functional governance ownership not present in source documents

**5. Summary Framing Rules**

**Lead with:** Process design expertise, operational problem-solving, and delivery in regulated clinical environments

**Do NOT lead with:** Cross-functional ownership, enterprise governance authority, or strategic process leadership

**Register:** Skilled practitioner who designs, implements, and improves processes and operating procedures in regulated environments --- with the technical and domain depth to solve real operational problems

**Length:** 3 to 4 sentences only

Required elements --- all four must be present:

-   Years of experience and domain (regulated clinical environments, pharma/biotech sponsor or CRO)

-   Core capability signal --- one of: process design, SOP development, operational efficiency, quality systems, or inspection readiness

-   Evidence of scope or complexity: types of processes worked on, functional areas engaged, or operational problems addressed

-   One forward-looking statement connecting Jason's process and operational experience to the specific need stated or implied in the job description

**3. Achievement Framing Rules**

Every achievement must follow this structure:

Operational problem or process gap -\> Specific process or framework designed or improved -\> Measurable or organizational outcome

Technical execution detail is appropriate at IC level. The specific process, procedure, or framework built or improved should be named. The outcome must be stated in concrete terms --- what improved, what risk was reduced, what was standardized --- not just what was produced.

Every achievement must be expressed within 2 lines. 3 lines is acceptable only for the highest-complexity achievements where scope or outcome detail cannot be compressed further. 4 or more lines is never acceptable.

**Calibration Examples**

**WEAK --- describes output, no problem or outcome:**

*\"Developed R scripts to analyze laboratory data submission trends and created dashboards for operational reporting.\"*

**STRONG --- operational problem first, specific solution, concrete outcome:**

*\"Redesigned external laboratory data acquisition and conformance procedures to address a near-50% submission rework rate, clarifying decision rights and embedding governance checkpoints into downstream processes. Rework reached zero within two submission cycles.\"*

**REFRAME --- same work, process contribution and outcome clearly stated:**

*\"Designed structured analytics and operational reporting for external laboratory submission oversight, enabling proactive conformance risk identification. Replaced a reactive review model with a governed, repeatable operating approach.\"*

**7. Content to De-emphasize**

-   Cross-functional ownership and enterprise governance language --- do not imply Jason owned the operating model, directed the governance structure, or held enterprise process authority. Reframe to the specific process, procedure, or framework he designed or improved.

-   Summary references to strategic process leadership --- the summary positions Jason as a skilled process practitioner, not a process strategy leader.

-   Organizational design signals --- role clarity, decision rights definition, and accountability model design are appropriate only if Jason's specific contribution was the design work, not organizational ownership of the outcome.

-   Technical tools section: include and give appropriate weight. Lean/Six Sigma tools, process modeling software, and clinical systems familiarity are relevant signals at IC level --- list accurately.
