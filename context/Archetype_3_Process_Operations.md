**ARCHETYPE 3: PROCESS & OPERATIONS**

*CV Generation Instruction Set --- Jason Delosh*

**1. Purpose and Scope**

This document is an instruction set for Phase 3 CV generation. Apply all rules in this document when the archetype identified in Phase 1 is Process & Operations. Do not apply these rules for any other archetype. If a dual-archetype was identified in Phase 1, apply primary framing from this document and secondary framing selectively within individual achievements only where both dimensions are genuinely present in the underlying experience.

**2. Archetype Match Criteria**

Confirm this is the correct archetype before proceeding. The role must center on one or more of the following:

-   Process design, standardization, or harmonization across clinical or regulated environments

-   Operational efficiency, Lean/Six Sigma, or continuous improvement leadership

-   Framework development, SOP governance, inspection readiness, or controlled documentation

-   Cross-functional process ownership with risk management or quality systems emphasis

-   CRO and vendor oversight models and external interface process management

If the role centers primarily on technology platform ownership, data strategy, or organizational transformation, verify the archetype assignment from Phase 1 before applying these instructions.

**3. Summary Framing Rules**

Write the professional summary using all of the following rules without exception:

**Lead with:** Process and governance expertise, cross-functional influence, and operational impact in regulated clinical environments

**Do NOT lead with:** Technical capabilities, data science credentials, programming languages, or analytical tools

**Register:** Strategic leader who builds scalable processes and governance structures --- not a technical executor who optimizes workflows

**Length:** 3 to 4 sentences only

Required elements --- all four must be present:

-   Years of experience and domain (regulated clinical environments, pharma/biotech sponsor or CRO)

-   Core capability signal --- one of: process design, governance, operational excellence, or inspection readiness

-   Evidence of scale or organizational impact (global, cross-functional, enterprise, or multi-program scope)

-   One forward-looking statement that connects Jason\'s capability to the specific need stated or implied in the job description

**4. Required Section Order**

Build the CV in exactly this section order. Do not reorder sections for this archetype:

-   1\. Professional Summary

-   2\. Core Competencies

-   3\. Professional Experience

-   4\. Education

-   5\. Certifications and Training

-   6\. Technical Proficiencies (last section --- condensed, minimal visual weight)

Core Competencies should list 8 to 12 items and prioritize process, governance, and operational capabilities. Technical tools and analytical capabilities appear only if space permits and should not dominate the list.

**5. Inventory Tag Priorities**

Query the Experience Inventory using these tag combinations in priority order. Each tier below defines the minimum representation required in the finished CV.

**High Priority --- Must Be Represented**

These tag combinations must map to at least one achievement in the finished CV. If fewer than two are coverable from the inventory, flag the gap explicitly before generating.

-   Capability: Process Design & Optimization --- Role Level: Senior Manager, Director, or Senior Director

-   Capability: Governance & Risk Management --- Context: Mature/Enterprise or Scaling

-   Capability: Stakeholder Management & Influence --- Role Level: Senior Manager, Director, or Senior Director

-   Outcome: Risk Reduction OR Outcome: Efficiency Gain (at least one must appear with a metric or organizational result)

**Medium Priority --- Include Where Space Permits**

-   Capability: Strategic Planning & Roadmapping --- only when the roadmap subject is process- or governance-focused, not technology-focused. Do not use technology roadmap entries for this archetype.

-   Capability: Change Management & Adoption --- Context: Scaling or Greenfield. Use to show process adoption at scale, not organizational transformation.

-   Outcome: Quality Improvement --- only if tied to a framework, governance structure, or SOP. Not applicable for data analysis outcomes.

**Use If Space Permits**

-   Capability: Data & Analytics --- only if the achievement produces a process or governance outcome. The analytical work must be in service of the process, not the end product itself.

-   Capability: Organizational Design --- only if tied to process ownership structure, role clarity in a process context, or decision rights definition.

**Avoid for This Archetype**

-   Capability: Technology Implementation --- exclude unless the technology directly enabled a process or governance capability. If included, the achievement must still lead with the process outcome.

-   IC-level achievements without a process or framework outcome --- reframe at appropriate level or exclude

-   Purely analytical achievements without operational or organizational impact

-   Training delivery unless it was enablement for a new capability that Jason designed and built

**6. Achievement Selection and Framing Rules**

**Selection Criteria**

Select an achievement only if it satisfies all three criteria:

-   Describes a process, framework, governance structure, operating model, or quality system --- not a data analysis output or technical tool build

-   States or clearly implies the business problem or operational risk that drove the work

-   Includes an organizational scope signal: cross-functional, multi-program, global, enterprise-wide, or explicit scale indicator

**Required Framing Pattern**

Every achievement must follow this structure. Deviations require explicit justification:

Business problem or operational risk -\> Process/framework/governance solution -\> Measurable or organizational outcome

The business problem must come first. Do not lead with the solution. Do not lead with technical execution. The outcome must be organizational or measurable --- not a description of an artifact produced.

**Calibration Examples**

**WEAK --- leads with technical execution, no process or governance framing:**

*\"Developed R scripts to analyze laboratory data submission trends and created dashboards for operational reporting.\"*

**STRONG --- business problem first, process solution, organizational outcome:**

*\"Eliminated recurring external laboratory data submission errors --- approaching a 50% rework rate --- by redesigning acquisition and conformance procedures, clarifying decision rights across three functional areas, and embedding governance oversight into downstream processes. Reduced rework to zero within two submission cycles.\"*

**REFRAME --- same underlying work, repositioned for this archetype:**

*\"Established data-driven quality surveillance for external laboratory submissions, designing analytics frameworks and operational reporting that enabled proactive identification of conformance risks and reduced submission rework. Converted an ad hoc, reactive process into a governed, repeatable operating model.\"*

**7. Content to De-emphasize**

Apply these rules throughout CV generation --- not just in the Technical section:

-   Technical skills section: include but keep short. List only; do not elaborate on proficiency levels or applications. This section must appear last.

-   Programming languages: list as comma-separated items only. No descriptions, no first-appearance dates, no application details.

-   Machine learning and modeling work: omit unless it directly produced a process or governance capability. If included, the achievement leads with the process outcome --- not the model.

-   Data science framing in the summary: route all data science and analytics language to the Data & Analytics archetype. If this archetype is active, the summary reads as an operations and process leader.

-   Tool-specific achievements: any achievement that is primarily about implementing or using a software tool belongs under Platform & Technology archetype, not this one.

**8. Pre-Generation Framing Checklist**

Verify every item before generating a single line of CV content under this archetype. If any item cannot be confirmed, resolve it before proceeding. Do not generate the CV with unresolved checklist failures.

-   Summary leads with process and governance expertise --- not technical or analytical capabilities

-   Summary contains all four required elements defined in Section 3

-   At least two achievements explicitly name a framework, governance structure, SOP, operating model, or quality system that was built or redesigned

-   Cross-functional scope is stated in at least two achievements (functions, teams, or programs affected)

-   At least one achievement includes a risk reduction or efficiency outcome with a metric or organizational result

-   No achievement reads as purely IC-level technical work without a process or governance framing

-   Technical skills section appears last and is limited to a brief list --- no elaboration

-   Section order matches the prescribed order in Section 4

-   Every achievement follows the business problem to solution to outcome framing pattern

-   No achievement leads with a tool, language, or analytical method
