# CV Content Rules — Individual Contributor Roles

> These are active content rules. Apply each rule while generating CV content. Compliance is demonstrated through what is generated, not confirmed in advance.

---

## Voice and Register

- Voice reflects individual contribution, technical execution, and subject matter expertise — not organizational leadership or strategic direction
- Framing highlights what was built, delivered, analyzed, or solved — and the impact of that work on the team, project, or organization
- Scope claims are accurate and appropriately scoped to the IC level — do not inflate to imply leadership, budget authority, or cross-functional ownership not present in source documents
- Seniority of voice matches the role level confirmed in Phase 2a — a senior IC reads differently from a junior IC in depth, independence, and complexity of work

---

## Achievement Rules

- Every achievement states what was done and why it mattered — technical execution is appropriate and expected at this level
- Outcome is stated in concrete terms: what was delivered, what improved, what risk was reduced, what problem was solved
- Quantified outcomes are included wherever available in source documents — do not invent metrics
- Technical depth is a strength at IC level — methods, tools, and approaches are relevant and should be represented accurately
- Self-initiated work and independent judgment are strong signals at IC level — surface these where present in source documents
- No inflated scope claims — do not imply team leadership, cross-functional ownership, or strategic authority not present in source documents

---

## Bullet Construction Framework

Apply the CCAR framework. For IC bullets, Context and Challenge are compressed into a single clause.

| Component | Definition |
|---|---|
| **Context/Challenge** | The situation, problem, or gap that drove the work. One clause is sufficient at IC level. |
| **Action** | What specifically was done. Methods, tools, and approaches are appropriate and expected at IC level. |
| **Result** | What improved, what risk was reduced, what was delivered. Concrete impact, not a description of the artifact produced. |

**Impact statement types — use the strongest available. Never use vague category labels (improved efficiency, enhanced quality, reduced risk — these are not impact statements).**

- Type 1 Quantified: hard numbers where genuinely known.
- Type 2 Bounded Qualitative: specific, verifiable claims. "First," "only," "eliminated," "established."
- Type 3 Contextual Narrative: before-state plus after-state. Most underused. Particularly useful for process and tooling work at IC level where hard metrics are unavailable but the change is unambiguous.

**When quantitative data is not available**, in order of preference: (1) bounded qualitative claim, (2) contextual narrative with before and after state, (3) proxy metrics such as adoption rate, timeline compression, or rework elimination, (4) scope as signal when outcome is genuinely unmeasurable.

---

## Bullet Formatting

- Target length: 2 lines per bullet
- 3 lines acceptable only for the highest-value complex achievements with significant technical or outcome detail
- 4 or more lines: never acceptable — compress or split
- Em-dashes prohibited as clause connectors in all bullet text and prose — rewrite as separate sentences or use natural connective language
- Em-dashes used as structural separators in company/location header lines are the only permitted exception

---

## Content Sourcing

- Every achievement and claim is traceable to one of three approved sources: a source document loaded in this session, information provided by the user during this session, or memory context explicitly confirmed by the user
- No content is invented or inferred without user confirmation
- If a gap cannot be closed from source material, flag it — do not fill it with creative framing

---

## Technical Content

- Technical skills, tools, and methods are appropriately prominent for IC roles — list fully and accurately per source documents
- Technical Proficiencies section carries more weight at IC level than at leadership level
- Specific languages, frameworks, environments, and tools are relevant signals — include them
- Hands-on execution detail is appropriate in achievement descriptions at this level

---

## Document Structure

- Section order follows the active archetype instruction set where applicable — IC roles may have reduced section complexity relative to leadership CVs
- Target length: 2-3 pages for most IC roles — do not pad to leadership CV length
- No unnecessary carriage returns — spacing is handled by formatting spec
