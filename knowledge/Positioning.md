# INDUSTRY TRAGECTORY --- AND WHERE I FIT

Clinical development is under pressure from four converging shifts:

**[AI is moving from exploration to execution.]{.underline}** Organizations have spent years piloting AI concepts. The expectation in 2026 is return on investment --- which requires governed, use-case-ready data foundations before AI can deliver anything. Organizations that haven't built those foundations are discovering that AI readiness is a prerequisite, not a byproduct of adoption.

**[Submission timelines are compressing.]{.underline}** Regulators are deploying their own AI-assisted review capabilities, and the expectation for faster, more reliable regulatory packages is increasing. Data quality and acquisition processes that were adequate for traditional timelines are becoming bottlenecks.

**[Trial complexity is increasing.]{.underline}** Targeted therapies, rare disease pipelines, and emerging FDA pathways for small-n trials mean smaller, more complex, higher-stakes data environments. Generalist operational models and legacy processes weren't designed for this level of specificity --- and stretching them produces diminishing returns.

**[Regulatory requirements are diverging globally.]{.underline}** Harmonized expectations across geographies are fraying. Data sovereignty requirements and diverging regulatory frameworks are forcing sponsors to rethink how data is collected, governed, and submitted across global programs.

My work sits directly at the intersection of all four.

-   Building AI-ready data foundations, eliminating submission rework

-   Designing operating models that scale to complexity without proportional headcount increases

-   Building governance frameworks that hold up in regulated environments

That is the work I have been doing, and it is the work the industry now requires at scale.
